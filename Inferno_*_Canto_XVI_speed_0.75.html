Inferno * Canto XVI

As Dante and Virgil near the edge of the seventh circle, they hear the roar of a great waterfall. Three shades, their bodies covered in burns, detach from a larger group running under the rain of fire and approach the travelers. They form a constantly moving wheel, keeping their faces turned toward Dante. One of them, Iacopo Rusticucci, identifies himself and his companions, Guido Guerra and Tegghiaio Aldobrandi, as prominent Florentines. They ask Dante for news of Florence, and he replies that the city is afflicted by new wealth and arrogance. The spirits accept this answer, ask to be remembered on Earth, and then quickly run away. Reaching the edge of a great cliff, Virgil instructs Dante to untie a cord from his waist. Virgil takes the cord and throws it into the chasm below. In response, a monstrous figure begins swimming up toward them from the dark abyss.

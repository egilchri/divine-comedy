Paradiso * Canto XXV

In the Sphere of the Fixed Stars, a new luminous soul emerges and joins Dante, Beatrice, and St. Peter. Beatrice identifies the light as the Apostle James. After St. Peter and St. James greet one another, Beatrice prompts St. James to examine Dante on the subject of Hope. Dante answers the apostle's questions, defining the virtue and citing his sources for it. Upon his successful response, the spirits sing, and a third, intensely bright light appears. Beatrice identifies this soul as the Apostle John. Dante stares at this new light, trying to discern the physical body he believed John took to Heaven. The effort of staring renders Dante temporarily blind. The voice of St. John corrects Dante, stating that his body remains on Earth. As the canto concludes, the celestial song and dance cease. Dante turns toward Beatrice but is distressed to find he is unable to see her.

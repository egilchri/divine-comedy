Purgatorio * Canto XXVII

At the final terrace, an angel informs Dante, Virgil, and Statius that they must pass through a wall of fire to proceed. Dante is overcome with fear, but Virgil reassures him that the flames can cause pain but not harm. When Dante remains immobile, Virgil states that this fire is the only thing separating him from Beatrice, which immediately gives Dante the courage to enter. Virgil leads the way, with Dante following and Statius behind them. The heat is immense. A singing voice guides them out of the fire and onto a stairway leading upward. As night falls, their progress is halted, and the three poets lie down to sleep on the steps. Dante dreams of a beautiful woman named Leah gathering flowers. He awakens at dawn, and they climb the last stairs. At the summit, Virgil announces his guidance is complete. He declares that Dante's will is now free, and he crowns Dante lord over himself.

And he said: "You have seen the temporal fire and the eternal, my son, and you have come to a place where, by myself, I discern no further. I have brought you here with intellect and with art; take your pleasure now as your guide; you are out of the steep paths, you are out of the art."

Inferno * Canto XXIII

Fearing pursuit by the Malebranche demons, Virgil snatches Dante and slides down a steep embankment into the sixth bolgia. The demons reach the ridge above but are unable to follow. In this new ditch, the poets encounter souls trudging in a slow procession, weighed down by heavy, lead-lined cloaks that are brilliantly gilded on the outside. Two of these sinners, identifying themselves as the friars Catalano and Loderingo from Bologna, stop to speak with the travelers. During their conversation, Dante notices a man crucified on the ground with three stakes, positioned so that all the other souls must walk over him. Catalano explains that this is Caiaphas, the high priest who counseled the Pharisees. Virgil then asks the friars for the path onward, and Catalano reveals that the bridge crossing this bolgia is broken, contradicting what the demon Malacoda had told them previously. Realizing he was deceived, Virgil departs in anger, with Dante following close behind.
Vòlt’ era in su la favola d’Isopo lo mio pensier per la presente rissa, dov’ el parlò de la rana e del topo; ché più non si pareggia ‘mo’ e ‘issa’ che l’un con l’altro fa, se ben s’accoppia principio e fine con la mente fissa.
My thoughts had turned to Aesop's fable, to this present quarrel, where he spoke of the frog and the mouse; for no more do ‘now’ and ‘then’ equal each other than the actions of these two, if one joins beginning and end with a fixed mind.

They wore cloaks with low hoods, pulled down over their eyes, fashioned in the style that is made for monks in Cluny. The outside is gilded, so that it dazzles; but inside, they are all lead, and so heavy that Frederick's were made of straw.

But through their weariness, those weary souls advanced so slowly that we, with every shift of their hips, seemed to be newcomers to their company. Because of this, I turned to my guide: "See if you can find someone who is known for their deeds or by name, and as we go, keep your eyes moving around."
E un che ’ntese la parola tosca, di retro a noi gridò: «Tenete i piedi, voi che correte sì per l’aura fosca! Forse ch’avrai da me quel che tu chiedi».
And one who understood the Tuscan tongue, called out behind us: "Hold your feet, you who run so swiftly through the dusky air! Perhaps you'll get from me what you're asking."
Onde ’l duca si volse e disse: «Aspetta, e poi secondo il suo passo procedi». Ristetti, e vidi due mostrar gran fretta de l’animo, col viso, d’esser meco; ma tardavali ’l carco e la via stretta.
Therefore my guide turned back and said: "Wait," and then proceed at his pace. I stopped, and saw two showing great haste of spirit, by their faces, to be with me; but the burden and the narrow road hindered them.

When they arrived, they eyed me with suspicion, not saying a word. Then they turned to themselves, muttering amongst themselves: "This one appears alive by the movement of his throat; and if they are dead, by what privilege do they walk uncovered by their heavy robes?"

Then he directed the following words to the friar: "Do not take offense, if you are allowed, to tell us if on the right hand lies any outlet by which we both can escape, without compelling the black angels to come from this abyss to separate us from it."
Rispuose adunque: «Più che tu non speri s’appressa un sasso che da la gran cerchia si move e varca tutt’ i vallon feri, salvo che ’n questo è rotto e nol coperchia; montar potrete su per la ruina, che giace in costa e nel fondo soperchia».
He then replied: "Closer than you think, a rock advances, which detaches from the great circle and traverses all the cruel valleys, except in this one, it is broken and does not cover it. You will be able to climb up the ruin that lies on its side and excessively in the depths."
Lo duca stette un poco a testa china; poi disse: «Mal contava la bisogna colui che i peccator di qua uncina». E ’l frate: «Io udi’ già dire a Bologna del diavol vizi assai, tra ’ quali udi’ ch’elli è bugiardo, e padre di menzogna».
The guide stood a moment, head bowed, then said: "That one told the tale badly, the one who hooks the sinners on this side." And the friar: "I have heard it said in Bologna of the devil's many vices, among which I heard he is a liar and the father of lies."
Appresso il duca a gran passi sen gì, turbato un poco d’ira nel sembiante; ond’ io da li ’ncarcati mi parti’ dietro a le poste de le care piante.
Following, the duke went on at a quick pace, a bit troubled by anger in his expression; so I departed from the burdened ones, following the footsteps of his cherished feet.

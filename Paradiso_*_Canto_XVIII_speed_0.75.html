Paradiso * Canto XVIII

In the Heaven of Mars, Dante’s conversation with a soul is interrupted by Beatrice. Another spirit then identifies other warrior souls within the great cross of light. As he names Joshua, Judas Maccabeus, Charlemagne, Roland, Godfrey of Bouillon, and Robert Guiscard, a corresponding light flashes along the cross. Dante and Beatrice then ascend to the sixth heaven, the Sphere of Jupiter. Dante becomes aware of the transition by observing Beatrice's increased radiance. In this new sphere, countless bright souls fly together like birds, arranging themselves to form letters. They spell out the Latin phrase "DILIGITE IUSTITIAM QUI IUDICATIS TERRAM" ("Love justice, you who judge the earth"). After the message is complete, the souls gather at the final letter 'M'. Thousands of other lights then descend upon this letter, and the entire formation of spirits reconfigures itself into the shape of a large, brilliant eagle.
Già si godeva solo del suo verbo quello specchio beato, e io gustava lo mio, temprando col dolce l’acerbo; e quella donna ch’a Dio mi menava disse: «Muta pensier; pensa ch’i’ sono presso a colui ch’ogne torto disgrava».
Already that blessed mirror was rejoicing solely in His Word, and I was savoring mine, tempering the bitter with the sweet; and that woman who was leading me to God said: "Change your thought; consider that I am near to Him who absolves all wrong."
Io mi rivolsi a l’amoroso suono del mio conforto; e qual io allor vidi ne li occhi santi amor, qui l’abbandono: non perch’ io pur del mio parlar diffidi, ma per la mente che non può redire sovra sé tanto, s’altri non la guidi.
I turned to the loving sound of my comfort, and I abandon here the vision of the holy love that I saw then in those blessed eyes: not because I doubt my own speech, but because my mind cannot return so far above itself, if it is not guided.
Tanto poss’ io di quel punto ridire, che, rimirando lei, lo mio affetto libero fu da ogne altro disire, fin che ’l piacere etterno, che diretto raggiava in Bëatrice, dal bel viso mi contentava col secondo aspetto.
I can say so much about that moment: gazing at her, my affection was freed from every other desire, until the eternal joy, which radiated directly from Beatrice’s beautiful face, satisfied me with its secondary aspect.
Vincendo me col lume d’un sorriso, ella mi disse: «Volgiti e ascolta; ché non pur ne’ miei occhi è paradiso». Come si vede qui alcuna volta l’affetto ne la vista, s’elli è tanto, che da lui sia tutta l’anima tolta,
Overcoming me with the light of a smile, she said to me: “Turn and listen, for paradise is not only in my eyes.” As affection is sometimes seen in a glance, if it is so great that the entire soul is taken by it,
così nel fiammeggiar del folgór santo, a ch’io mi volsi, conobbi la voglia in lui di ragionarmi ancora alquanto. El cominciò: «In questa quinta soglia de l’albero che vive de la cima e frutta sempre e mai non perde foglia,
so in the flashing of the holy light, when I turned, I recognized his desire to speak with me a while longer. He began: “In this fifth terrace of the tree that lives from its crown and always bears fruit and never loses a leaf,
spiriti son beati, che giù, prima che venissero al ciel, fuor di gran voce, sì ch’ogne musa ne sarebbe opima. Però mira ne’ corni de la croce: quello ch’io nomerò, lì farà l’atto che fa in nube il suo foco veloce».
Spirits are blessed here, who, beforethey came to Heaven, cried out with such great voicethat every Muse would be enriched. Therefore, look to the horns of the cross:the one I name will perform there the actthat his swift fire does in a cloud.”
Io vidi per la croce un lume tratto dal nomar Iosuè, com’ el si feo; né mi fu noto il dir prima che ’l fatto. E al nome de l’alto Macabeo vidi moversi un altro roteando, e letizia era ferza del paleo.
I saw, drawn along the cross, a lightfrom the naming of Joshua, as he enacted it.Nor was the word known to me before the deed.And at the name of the great MaccabeeI saw another moving in a circle,and joy was the whip of that dance.
Così per Carlo Magno e per Orlando due ne seguì lo mio attento sguardo, com’ occhio segue suo falcon volando. Poscia trasse Guiglielmo e Rinoardo e ’l duca Gottifredi la mia vista per quella croce, e Ruberto Guiscardo.
Thus my intent gaze followed two more, for Charlemagne and for Roland, as the eye follows its falcon in flight. Then that cross drew William and Renouard, and Duke Godfrey, and Robert Guiscard.
Indi, tra l’altre luci mota e mista, mostrommi l’alma che m’avea parlato qual era tra i cantor del cielo artista. Io mi rivolsi dal mio destro lato per vedere in Beatrice il mio dovere, o per parlare o per atto, segnato;
Then, among the other lights, moving and mingled, she showed me the soul that had spoken to me, the artist among the singers of Heaven. I turned from my right side to see my duty in Beatrice, whether by speaking or by a sign.
e vidi le sue luci tanto mere, tanto gioconde, che la sua sembianza vinceva li altri e l’ultimo solere. E come, per sentir più dilettanza bene operando, l’uom di giorno in giorno s’accorge che la sua virtute avanza,
And I saw her lights so pure, so joyful, that her appearance surpassed the others and their usual state. And as a man, through good deeds, senses day by day an increase in his virtue, for greater pleasure…
sì m’accors’ io che ’l mio girare intorno col cielo insieme avea cresciuto l’arco, veggendo quel miracol più addorno. E qual è ’l trasmutare in picciol varco di tempo in bianca donna, quando ’l volto suo si discarchi di vergogna il carco,
Then I noticed that my circling along with the heavens had grown the arc, seeing that miracle more adorned. And as is the transmutation in a brief span of time into a fair woman, when her face unburdens itself of the weight of shame,
tal fu ne li occhi miei, quando fui vòlto, per lo candor de la temprata stella sesta, che dentro a sé m’avea ricolto. Io vidi in quella giovïal facella lo sfavillar de l’amor che lì era segnare a li occhi miei nostra favella.
Such was it in my eyes, when I had turned, because of the brightness of the tempered sixth star, which within itself had gathered me. I saw in that Jovian torch the flashing of the love that was there, marking our speech to my eyes.
E come augelli surti di rivera, quasi congratulando a lor pasture, fanno di sé or tonda or altra schiera, sì dentro ai lumi sante creature volitando cantavano, e faciensi or D, or I, or L in sue figure.
And as birds, newly risen from the riverbank, as if rejoicing in their sustenance, form now one shape, now another, so within those lights the holy creatures, flying, sang, and shaped themselves now into D's, now I's, now L's.
Prima, cantando, a sua nota moviensi; poi, diventando l’un di questi segni, un poco s’arrestavano e taciensi. O diva Pegasëa che li ’ngegni fai glorïosi e rendili longevi, ed essi teco le cittadi e ’ regni,
First, in song, they moved to their notes; then, becoming one of these letters, they paused a little and fell silent. O divine Pegasëa, who makes ingenious minds glorious and renders them long-lived, and they with you the cities and kingdoms,
illustrami di te, sì ch’io rilevi le lor figure com’ io l’ho concette: paia tua possa in questi versi brevi! Mostrarsi dunque in cinque volte sette vocali e consonanti; e io notai le parti sì, come mi parver dette.
Illuminate me with your light, so that I may bring forth their forms as I have conceived them. Let your power be evident in these brief verses! They appeared then, in five times seven vowels and consonants; and I noted the parts, as they seemed to be spoken to me.
‘DILIGITE IUSTITIAM’, primai fur verbo e nome di tutto ’l dipinto; ‘QUI IUDICATIS TERRAM’, fur sezzai. Poscia ne l’emme del vocabol quinto rimasero ordinate; sì che Giove pareva argento lì d’oro distinto.
“LOVE JUSTICE,” the firstword and name of the entire painting;“YOU WHO JUDGE THE EARTH,” the second. Then in the M of the fifth wordthey remained arranged; such that Joveseemed silver there, distinct from gold.
E vidi scendere altre luci dove era il colmo de l’emme, e lì quetarsi cantando, credo, il ben ch’a sé le move. Poi, come nel percuoter d’i ciocchi arsi surgono innumerabili faville, onde li stolti sogliono agurarsi,
And I saw other lights descend where the crest of the M was, and there they settled, singing, I believe, of the good that draws them to itself. Then, as in the striking of burning logs innumerable sparks arise, from which fools are accustomed to take omens,
resurger parver quindi più di mille luci e salir, qual assai e qual poco, sì come ’l sol che l’accende sortille; e quïetata ciascuna in suo loco, la testa e ’l collo d’un’aguglia vidi rappresentare a quel distinto foco.
Then, more than a thousand little lights resurged, and rose, some more, some less, as the sun, which ignites them, had assigned. And, each one settling in its place, I saw the head and neck of an eagle represented in that distinct fire.
Quei che dipinge lì, non ha chi ’l guidi; ma esso guida, e da lui si rammenta quella virtù ch’è forma per li nidi. L’altra bëatitudo, che contenta pareva prima d’ingigliarsi a l’emme, con poco moto seguitò la ’mprenta.
He who paints there has no guide; but he guides himself, and from him remembers that virtue which is the form for nests. The other blessedness, which seemed content at first to be imprinted with the M, with little movement followed the imprint.
O dolce stella, quali e quante gemme mi dimostraro che nostra giustizia effetto sia del ciel che tu ingemme! Per ch’io prego la mente in che s’inizia tuo moto e tua virtute, che rimiri ond’ esce il fummo che ’l tuo raggio vizia;
O sweet star, how many gems you showed me,and what their worth, to prove our justiceis born of heaven, which jewels you.For this I pray the mind in which beginsyour motion and your virtue, that it heedthe smoke from which your ray is fouled.
sì ch’un’altra fïata omai s’adiri del comperare e vender dentro al templo che si murò di segni e di martìri. O milizia del ciel cu’ io contemplo, adora per color che sono in terra tutti svïati dietro al malo essemplo!
So that another time it will be angeredby the buying and selling within the temple,which was built with signs and martyrdoms.Oh, militia of heaven that I behold,pray for those on earthwho are all led astray by the evil example!
Già si solea con le spade far guerra; ma or si fa togliendo or qui or quivi lo pan che ’l pïo Padre a nessun serra. Ma tu che sol per cancellare scrivi, pensa che Pietro e Paulo, che moriro per la vigna che guasti, ancor son vivi.
Once, wars were fought with swords; but now, they're fought by taking, now here, now there, the bread that the pious Father denies to no one. But you, who write only to erase, consider that Peter and Paul, who died for the vineyard you ravage, are still alive.
Ben puoi tu dire: «I’ ho fermo ’l disiro sì a colui che volle viver solo e che per salti fu tratto al martiro, ch’io non conosco il pescator né Polo».
Then you can say: "I have fixed my desire on him who wanted to live alone, and who was torn to martyrdom in leaps, such that I do not know the fisherman nor Paul."

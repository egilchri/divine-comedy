Paradiso * Canto XV

In the Heaven of Mars, the souls of warriors, arranged in a radiant cross, fall silent. A single bright spirit detaches itself from the arm of the cross and descends towards Dante. Speaking in Latin, the soul greets Dante as his own blood. Awed, Dante looks to Beatrice, who encourages him with a smile. Dante asks the spirit to reveal its name. The soul identifies himself as Cacciaguida, Dante’s great-great-grandfather. He describes the Florence of his time as a city of peace, sobriety, and simple virtues, contrasting it with the city of Dante’s era. Cacciaguida then recounts his personal history: he was born in Florence, married a woman from the Po Valley (from which the Alighieri surname derived), and served the Emperor Conrad. He was knighted for his service and followed the emperor on the Second Crusade, where he was killed in battle by the "iniquitous" people, a death he describes as his martyrdom.

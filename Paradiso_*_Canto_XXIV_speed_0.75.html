Paradiso * Canto XXIV

In the Sphere of the Fixed Stars, Beatrice invites the triumphant souls to share their wisdom with Dante. A brilliant light emerges from their ranks, circles Beatrice three times, and is identified by her as the apostle Saint Peter. At Beatrice's request, Saint Peter begins to examine Dante on the subject of faith. Peter first asks for a definition of faith, which Dante provides. He then asks if Dante possesses this faith and from where he received it. Dante affirms that he does and that its source is the Old and New Testaments. When questioned on the proof of scripture’s divine origin, Dante cites the miracles described within and the subsequent conversion of the world. After the other souls sing in praise, Dante professes the content of his belief in one God and the Trinity. Pleased with the examination, the light of Saint Peter circles Dante three times in a sung blessing before the canto ends.

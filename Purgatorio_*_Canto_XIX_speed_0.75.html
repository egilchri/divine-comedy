Purgatorio * Canto XIX

Just before dawn, Dante dreams of a deformed, stuttering woman who transforms into a beautiful, singing siren under his gaze. A holy lady appears and calls to Virgil, who seizes the siren, tears open her garments, and exposes her foul stomach, the stench from which awakens Dante. Virgil leads the pensive Dante onward. An angel with swan-like wings directs them to the next ascent, brushing a "P" from Dante's brow while reciting the Beatitude for those who mourn. They arrive on the fifth terrace and see souls lying face-down on the ground, bound hand and foot, weeping and reciting a psalm. These are the avaricious and the prodigal. One of the souls identifies himself as Pope Adrian V. He explains that he was consumed with avarice for earthly power until his election as pope revealed its vanity. His punishment is to be fixed to the ground, just as his heart was once fixed on worldly goods. When Dante kneels out of reverence, Adrian V commands him to stand, stating that all are equal servants of God. He then dismisses Dante so he can continue his penance.

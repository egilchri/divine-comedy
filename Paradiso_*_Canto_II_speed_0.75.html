Paradiso * Canto II

The canto begins with the narrator addressing his readers, warning those not equipped for the intellectual journey ahead to turn back. He and Beatrice then ascend to the first celestial sphere, the Moon, with incredible speed. Beatrice encourages him to give thanks to God upon their arrival. Dante describes being enveloped by the substance of the Moon, which appears as a luminous and solid cloud. He questions Beatrice about the cause of the dark spots visible on the Moon's surface from Earth, proposing that they result from variations in the body's density. Beatrice systematically refutes this hypothesis with logical arguments and a proposed experiment involving three mirrors and a light source. She then begins her own explanation, stating that the different appearances of light are caused by a formal principle—a distinct virtue—which is distributed throughout the celestial bodies from a higher divine source, not by physical rarity or density.

You few, who raised your necks in time for the bread of angels, that which is eaten here, but does not satisfy, you may well launch your ship upon the deep, following the wake I have made before the water that returns to its usual state.
Que’ glorïosi che passaro al Colco non s’ammiraron come voi farete, quando Iasón vider fatto bifolco. La concreata e perpetüa sete del deïforme regno cen portava veloci quasi come ’l ciel vedete.
How glorious those who passed to Colchis did not marvel as you will, when Jason saw himself become a plowman. The inborn and perpetual thirst of the misshapen realm carried us swiftly, almost as quickly as you see the sky.
Beatrice in suso, e io in lei guardava; e forse in tanto in quanto un quadrel posa e vola e da la noce si dischiava, giunto mi vidi ove mirabil cosa mi torse il viso a sé; e però quella cui non potea mia cura essere ascosa,
Beatrice above, and I looked on her; and perhaps in the time it takes an arrow to rest and fly, and to leave the nock, I found myself where a wondrous thing drew my gaze; and thus she, to whom my concern could not be hidden,
volta ver’ me, sì lieta come bella, «Drizza la mente in Dio grata», mi disse, «che n’ha congiunti con la prima stella». Parev’ a me che nube ne coprisse lucida, spessa, solida e pulita, quasi adamante che lo sol ferisse.
She turned toward me, as joyous as she was beautiful. "Turn your mind to God in gratitude," she said, "for He has joined us with the first star." It seemed to me a cloud covered us, shining, dense, solid, and pure, like a diamond struck by the sun.
Per entro sé l’etterna margarita ne ricevette, com’ acqua recepe raggio di luce permanendo unita. S’io era corpo, e qui non si concepe com’ una dimensione altra patio, ch’esser convien se corpo in corpo repe,
Within herself, the eternal pearl received me, as water receives a ray of light, remaining united. If I was a body, and here it is not conceived how one dimension could contain another, which must be if body within body does penetrate,
accender ne dovria più il disio di veder quella essenza in che si vede come nostra natura e Dio s’unio. Lì si vedrà ciò che tenem per fede, non dimostrato, ma fia per sé noto a guisa del ver primo che l’uom crede.
It should further ignite my desire to see that essence in which is seen how our nature and God are united. There shall be seen what we hold by faith, not demonstrated, but known of itself, in the manner of the first truth that man believes.
Io rispuosi: «Madonna, sì devoto com’ esser posso più, ringrazio lui lo qual dal mortal mondo m’ha remoto. Ma ditemi: che son li segni bui di questo corpo, che là giuso in terra fan di Cain favoleggiare altrui?».
I responded: "My lady, as devoted as I can be, I thank Him who has removed me from the mortal world. But tell me: what are the dark marks on this body that make those below on Earth tell tales of Cain?"
Ella sorrise alquanto, e poi «S’elli erra l’oppinïon», mi disse, «d’i mortali dove chiave di senso non diserra, certo non ti dovrien punger li strali d’ammirazione omai, poi dietro ai sensi vedi che la ragione ha corte l’ali.
She smiled a little, and then said to me, "If the opinion of mortals errs, where the key of understanding does not unlock, certainly the barbs of wonder should not sting you now, since you see reason has short wings behind the senses.
Ma dimmi quel che tu da te ne pensi». E io: «Ciò che n’appar qua sù diverso credo che fanno i corpi rari e densi».
"But tell me what you yourself think about it." And I: "What appears to us up here as different, I believe is made by rare and dense bodies."
Ed ella: «Certo assai vedrai sommerso nel falso il creder tuo, se bene ascolti l’argomentar ch’io li farò avverso. La spera ottava vi dimostra molti lumi, li quali e nel quale e nel quanto notar si posson di diversi volti.
And she said: "Surely you will see your belief submerged in falsehood if you listen closely to the arguments I will make against it. The eighth sphere reveals to you many lights, which you can mark in both their quality and quantity through different appearances.
Se raro e denso ciò facesser tanto, una sola virtù sarebbe in tutti, più e men distributa e altrettanto. Virtù diverse esser convegnon frutti di princìpi formali, e quei, for ch’uno, seguiterieno a tua ragion distrutti.
If they were so rare and dense, a single virtue would exist in all, distributed in greater and lesser measure. Different virtues must be the fruits of formal principles, and, save one, these principles would be destroyed by your reasoning.
Ancor, se raro fosse di quel bruno cagion che tu dimandi, o d’oltre in parte fora di sua materia sì digiuno esto pianeto, o, sì come comparte lo grasso e ’l magro un corpo, così questo nel suo volume cangerebbe carte.
Even if the cause of that darkness were rare, as you inquire, this planet would either be lacking in its matter in some part, or, just as a body shares both fat and lean, so would this planet’s volume change its composition.
Se ’l primo fosse, fora manifesto ne l’eclissi del sol, per trasparere lo lume come in altro raro ingesto. Questo non è: però è da vedere de l’altro; e s’elli avvien ch’io l’altro cassi, falsificato fia lo tuo parere.
If the first were true, it would be evident in the sun's eclipse, the light shining through like something else that is rare. This is not the case; therefore, we must consider the other possibility, and if I disprove that second option, your idea will be proven false.
S’elli è che questo raro non trapassi, esser conviene un termine da onde lo suo contrario più passar non lassi; e indi l’altrui raggio si rifonde così come color torna per vetro lo qual di retro a sé piombo nasconde.
If this rare thing does not pass through, there must be a boundary beyond which its opposite can no longer pass; and from that point, the other ray is reflected, just as a color returns through glass that hides lead behind it.
Or dirai tu ch’el si dimostra tetro ivi lo raggio più che in altre parti, per esser lì refratto più a retro. Da questa instanza può deliberarti esperïenza, se già mai la provi, ch’esser suol fonte ai rivi di vostr’ arti.
Or, will you say that the ray appears darker there than elsewhere, because it is reflected further back? Experience can resolve you of this doubt, if you ever test it, for it is often the source of the streams of your arts.
Tre specchi prenderai; e i due rimovi da te d’un modo, e l’altro, più rimosso, tr’ambo li primi li occhi tuoi ritrovi. Rivolto ad essi, fa che dopo il dosso ti stea un lume che i tre specchi accenda e torni a te da tutti ripercosso.
You will take three mirrors; remove the two from you at equal distances, and with the third, more distant, find your eyes between the first two. Turning toward them, place a light behind your back so that it ignites the three mirrors and returns to you, reflected from all.
Ben che nel quanto tanto non si stenda la vista più lontana, lì vedrai come convien ch’igualmente risplenda. Or, come ai colpi de li caldi rai de la neve riman nudo il suggetto e dal colore e dal freddo primai,
Even though your farthest vision does not reach as far, there you will see how it is right that it shines equally. Now, just as the subject remains bare of both color and former cold in the blows of the warm rays of the sun upon the snow,
così rimaso te ne l’intelletto voglio informar di luce sì vivace, che ti tremolerà nel suo aspetto. Dentro dal ciel de la divina pace si gira un corpo ne la cui virtute l’esser di tutto suo contento giace.
So I want to inform your intellectwith light so vibrantthat you'll tremble in its presence.Within the heaven of divine peacea body spins, in whose virtuelies the being of all its contentment.
Lo ciel seguente, c’ha tante vedute, quell’ esser parte per diverse essenze, da lui distratte e da lui contenute. Li altri giron per varie differenze le distinzion che dentro da sé hanno dispongono a lor fini e lor semenze.
The following heaven, which has so many sights, divides that being into diverse essences, detached from it, yet contained within it. The other circles, through various differences, arrange within themselves the distinctions that dispose towards their ends and their seeds.
Questi organi del mondo così vanno, come tu vedi omai, di grado in grado, che di sù prendono e di sotto fanno. Riguarda bene omai sì com’ io vado per questo loco al vero che disiri, sì che poi sappi sol tener lo guado.
These instruments of the world proceed, as you now see, step by step, taking from above and creating below. Observe carefully now how I proceed through this place towards the truth you desire, so that you may then know only to keep the ford.
Lo moto e la virtù d’i santi giri, come dal fabbro l’arte del martello, da’ beati motor convien che spiri; e ’l ciel cui tanti lumi fanno bello, de la mente profonda che lui volve prende l’image e fassene suggello.
The motion and the power of the holy spheres, as a hammer's art from a smith, must come from blessed movers; and the heaven that so many lights make beautiful, from the deep mind that turns it takes the image and makes of it a seal.
E come l’alma dentro a vostra polve per differenti membra e conformate a diverse potenze si risolve, così l’intelligenza sua bontate multiplicata per le stelle spiega, girando sé sovra sua unitate.
And as the soul within your dust, through differing limbs, formed in a way that suits varying strengths, resolves itself, in the same way, intelligence spreads its goodness, multiplied through the stars, turning itself upon its own unity.
Virtù diversa fa diversa lega col prezïoso corpo ch’ella avviva, nel qual, sì come vita in voi, si lega. Per la natura lieta onde deriva, la virtù mista per lo corpo luce come letizia per pupilla viva.
Different virtues create different bondswith the precious bodies they enliven,to which they are bound, as life is bound to you.Because of the joyful nature from which they come,mixed virtue shines through the bodylike joy through a living pupil.
Da essa vien ciò che da luce a luce par differente, non da denso e raro; essa è formal principio che produce, conforme a sua bontà, lo turbo e ’l chiaro».
From it comes that which gives light to light,appearing different, not by density or rarity;it is the formal principle that produces,according to its goodness, the turbid and the clear."

Inferno * Canto XI

Dante and Virgil arrive at the edge of a steep precipice overlooking the seventh circle. The air is filled with a terrible stench, forcing them to seek shelter behind a large tomb. On the tomb’s lid, an inscription identifies it as belonging to Pope Anastasius. Virgil suggests they wait for their senses to acclimate to the foul odor before descending. To pass the time, he explains the layout of Lower Hell. He describes how the remaining three circles are organized by the severity of sin, starting with the seventh circle for violence, which is subdivided into three rings. Below that are the eighth and ninth circles for the fraudulent. Dante asks why the sinners they have already encountered are punished outside the city of Dis. Virgil explains the distinction between sins of incontinence and those of malice. After answering a final question about usury, Virgil notes the time by the stars and announces they must continue their journey.

Because fraud is a vice specific to humanity, it is more displeasing to God. Therefore, the fraudulent are beneath, and more pain assails them. The first circle is entirely for the violent; but because violence is done against three entities, it is divided and constructed into three rounds.

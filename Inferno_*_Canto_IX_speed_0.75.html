Inferno * Canto IX

Dante and Virgil wait before the locked gates of the city of Dis. To calm Dante's fear, Virgil recounts a previous time he descended this deep into Hell. Suddenly, three Furies—Megaera, Alecto, and Tisiphone—appear on a tower, shrieking and clawing at their chests. They threaten Dante and call for Medusa to turn him to stone. Virgil immediately orders Dante to turn away and covers the pilgrim's eyes with his own hands for protection. A powerful sound comes from across the Stygian marsh as an angelic messenger approaches, walking over the water. The damned souls flee before him. The messenger reaches the gate of Dis, rebukes the demons for their insolence, and touches the gate with a small wand, causing it to swing open. He then departs without speaking to the poets. Dante and Virgil enter the city, where they find a broad plain filled with open, fiery tombs from which laments can be heard. Virgil explains that this is the Sixth Circle, where heretics are punished.

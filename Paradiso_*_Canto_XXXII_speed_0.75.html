Paradiso * Canto XXXII

Saint Bernard, acting as Dante’s guide, explains the arrangement of the blessed souls within the Celestial Rose. He first points to the Virgin Mary, then identifies Eve seated at her feet. Below them, he indicates Rachel, who sits with Beatrice. Continuing down one side of the rose, Bernard names Sarah, Rebecca, Judith, and Ruth. He then describes the rose’s primary division: Old Testament believers who had faith in Christ’s coming sit on one side, while New Testament believers sit on the other. He also explains a horizontal division, noting that the lower tiers are occupied by the souls of children. As Dante observes Mary, the Archangel Gabriel appears before her and sings “Ave, Maria.” Bernard identifies Adam and Saint Peter seated on either side of Mary, with Moses and Saint John the Evangelist beside them, respectively. Opposite them sit Saint Anne and Lucy. Concluding his explanation, Bernard instructs Dante to prepare for the final vision of God and begins a prayer to Mary to ask for her assistance.

I saw so much joy rain down upon her, carried in the holy minds created to fly across that height, that, although I had seen much before, it did not suspend me in such admiration, nor did it show me so much of God.

And that love, which first descended there, singing "Hail, Mary, full of grace," spread its wings before her. The blessed court responded to the divine song from all sides, so that every sight became more serene.

"O holy father, who for my sake endures being here below, leaving the sweet place where you sit by eternal fate, who is that angel who with such joy gazes into the eyes of our queen, so enamored that he seems aflame?".

Those two who sit up there, most fortunate for being closest to Augusta, are like two roots of this rose: the one who joins it on the left is the father, by whose bold taste the human species tastes so much bitterness.

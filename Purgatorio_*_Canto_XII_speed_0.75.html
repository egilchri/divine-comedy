Purgatorio * Canto XII

Dante, who had been walking bent over, is instructed by his guide, Virgil, to straighten up and continue their journey. Virgil directs Dante’s attention to the pavement, which is intricately carved with reliefs depicting figures and scenes. Among the images are Lucifer falling from heaven, the giant Briareus struck down, Nimrod at his tower, Niobe with her dead children, Saul fallen on his sword, and Arachne transformed. After they view the carvings, Virgil points out an approaching angel. The bright, white-clad angel welcomes the poets and invites them to ascend the stairway to the next terrace. Before they proceed, the angel strikes Dante’s forehead with his wings. As Dante and Virgil climb the steps, voices sing "Beati pauperes spiritu." Dante feels suddenly lighter and asks why the ascent is so easy. Virgil explains that one of the seven 'P's on his forehead has been erased. Dante touches his brow and confirms that only six letters remain.
Di pari, come buoi che vanno a giogo, m’andava io con quell’ anima carca, fin che ’l sofferse il dolce pedagogo. Ma quando disse: «Lascia lui e varca; ché qui è buono con l’ali e coi remi, quantunque può, ciascun pinger sua barca»;
Likewise, as oxen go in yoke, I went with that burdened soul, until the sweet pedagogue allowed it. But when he said: "Leave him and pass on; for here it is good that each one, with wings and oars, pushes his boat as far as he can."
dritto sì come andar vuolsi rife’mi con la persona, avvegna che i pensieri mi rimanessero e chinati e scemi. Io m’era mosso, e seguia volontieri del mio maestro i passi, e amendue già mostravam com’ eravam leggeri;
Straight as one must go, I made myself again in person, though my thoughts remained both bent and diminished. I had moved, and willingly followed my master’s steps, and both of us already showed how light we were.
ed el mi disse: «Volgi li occhi in giùe: buon ti sarà, per tranquillar la via, veder lo letto de le piante tue». Come, perché di lor memoria sia, sovra i sepolti le tombe terragne portan segnato quel ch’elli eran pria,
And he said to me: "Cast your eyes downward; it will be good for you, to calm the path, to see the bed of your feet." Just as, so that there may be memory of them, upon the sepulchers, earthen tombs bear marked that which they were before.
onde lì molte volte si ripiagne per la puntura de la rimembranza, che solo a’ pïi dà de le calcagne; sì vid’ io lì, ma di miglior sembianza secondo l’artificio, figurato quanto per via di fuor del monte avanza.
Where, many times, lamentation rises up,stung by remembrance,which strikes only at the heels of the pious;so I saw there, but in a better guise,figured, according to the craftsman's skill,all that advances along the mountain's path.
Vedea colui che fu nobil creato più ch’altra creatura, giù dal cielo folgoreggiando scender, da l’un lato. Vedëa Brïareo fitto dal telo celestïal giacer, da l’altra parte, grave a la terra per lo mortal gelo.
I saw him who was nobly created, more than any other creature, descending from the sky, blazing like lightning, from one side. I saw Briareus, struck by the celestial dart, lying on the other side, heavy to the earth with the mortal chill.
Vedea Timbreo, vedea Pallade e Marte, armati ancora, intorno al padre loro, mirar le membra d’i Giganti sparte. Vedea Nembròt a piè del gran lavoro quasi smarrito, e riguardar le genti che ’n Sennaàr con lui superbi fuoro.
She saw Timbreo, she saw Pallas and Mars, still armed, gathered around their father, beholding the scattered limbs of the Giants. She saw Nimrod, almost bewildered, at the foot of the great work, and watching the people who, with him, had been arrogant in Shinar.
O Nïobè, con che occhi dolenti vedea io te segnata in su la strada, tra sette e sette tuoi figliuoli spenti! O Saùl, come in su la propria spada quivi parevi morto in Gelboè, che poi non sentì pioggia né rugiada!
O Niobe, with what sorrowful eyes did I see you depicted on the road, among your dead sons, seven and seven! O Saul, how did you appear there, dead upon your own sword in Gelboe, a place that later felt neither rain nor dew!
O folle Aragne, sì vedea io te già mezza ragna, trista in su li stracci de l’opera che mal per te si fé. O Roboàm, già non par che minacci quivi ’l tuo segno; ma pien di spavento nel porta un carro, sanza ch’altri il cacci.
O mad Arachne, I saw you there, already half spider, wretched on the remnants of the tapestry you wove, which brought you grief. O Rehoboam, your threats are not displayed there now; but full of terror, you drive a chariot, though no one pursues you.
Mostrava ancor lo duro pavimento come Almeon a sua madre fé caro parer lo sventurato addornamento. Mostrava come i figli si gittaro sovra Sennacherìb dentro dal tempio, e come, morto lui, quivi il lasciaro.
It showed as well the hard pavement, how Alcmaeon made his mother’s adornment seem precious to her, a wretched thing. It showed how his sons threw themselves upon Sennacherib within the temple, and how, after he was dead, they left him there.
Mostrava la ruina e ’l crudo scempio che fé Tamiri, quando disse a Ciro: «Sangue sitisti, e io di sangue t’empio». Mostrava come in rotta si fuggiro li Assiri, poi che fu morto Oloferne, e anche le reliquie del martiro.
It showed the ruin and the cruel slaughter that Tamiris made, when she said to Cyrus: "You thirsted for blood, and I give you your fill of blood." It showed how the Assyrians fled in defeat after Holofernes was dead, and also the relics of the martyrdom.
Vedeva Troia in cenere e in caverne; o Ilïón, come te basso e vile mostrava il segno che lì si discerne! Qual di pennel fu maestro o di stile che ritraesse l’ombre e ’ tratti ch’ivi mirar farieno uno ingegno sottile?
It showed Troy in ashes and in ruins; o Ilion, how low and vile the sign it showed was discerned there! What master of brush or style was there to portray the shadows and the features there that would make one marvel at a subtle mind?
Morti li morti e i vivi parean vivi: non vide mei di me chi vide il vero, quant’ io calcai, fin che chinato givi. Or superbite, e via col viso altero, figliuoli d’Eva, e non chinate il volto sì che veggiate il vostro mal sentero!
The dead were dead, and the living seemed alive: no one saw the truth better than I did, as I trod that ground, until I bent low. Now be proud, and with haughty faces, children of Eve, do not bow your faces so you see your ill path!
Più era già per noi del monte vòlto e del cammin del sole assai più speso che non stimava l’animo non sciolto, quando colui che sempre innanzi atteso andava, cominciò: «Drizza la testa; non è più tempo di gir sì sospeso.
More of the mountain was already turned for us, and more of the sun's journey had been spent than my unburdened spirit had thought, when he who always walked ahead began, "Lift up your head; there is no longer time to walk so pensively."
Vedi colà un angel che s’appresta per venir verso noi; vedi che torna dal servigio del dì l’ancella sesta. Di reverenza il viso e li atti addorna, sì che i diletti lo ’nvïarci in suso; pensa che questo dì mai non raggiorna!».
"Look, there's an angel coming this way to meet us. See, the sixth handmaid is returning from her service of the day. Show reverence in your face and your bearing, so that he may guide us upward. Consider that this day will never dawn again!"
Io era ben del suo ammonir uso pur di non perder tempo, sì che ’n quella materia non potea parlarmi chiuso. A noi venìa la creatura bella, biancovestito e ne la faccia quale par tremolando mattutina stella.
I heeded well her warning, not to waste time, so that on this matter she could not speak to me in riddles. Toward us came the beautiful creature, clad in white, and in his face was a light like a trembling morning star.
Le braccia aperse, e indi aperse l’ale; disse: «Venite: qui son presso i gradi, e agevolemente omai si sale. A questo invito vegnon molto radi: o gente umana, per volar sù nata, perché a poco vento così cadi?».
He spread his arms, and then his wings; he said, "Come: here the steps are close, and now you can climb easily." Few heed this invitation: O human race, born to fly upward, why do you fall at the slightest breeze?"
Menocci ove la roccia era tagliata; quivi mi batté l’ali per la fronte; poi mi promise sicura l’andata. Come a man destra, per salire al monte dove siede la chiesa che soggioga la ben guidata sopra Rubaconte,
He then showed me where the rock had been carved; there he beat his wings upon my brow; then he promised me a safe passage. As to the right, in order to ascend the mountain where sits the church that dominates the well-guided above Rubaconte,
si rompe del montar l’ardita foga per le scalee che si fero ad etade ch’era sicuro il quaderno e la doga; così s’allenta la ripa che cade quivi ben ratta da l’altro girone; ma quinci e quindi l’alta pietra rade.
There, the mountain's bold surge breaks apart,by the stairs that were fashioned in an agewhen the ledger and the barrel were secure;so the bank, which falls swiftly therefrom the other circle, loosens;but here and there the high stone grazes.
Noi volgendo ivi le nostre persone, ‘Beati pauperes spiritu!’ voci cantaron sì, che nol diria sermone. Ahi quanto son diverse quelle foci da l’infernali!
Turning our bodies there, the voices sang, "Blessed are the poor in spirit!" in a way that words cannot express. Ah, how different those welcomes are from Hell's!
ché quivi per canti s’entra, e là giù per lamenti feroci. Già montavam su per li scaglion santi, ed esser mi parea troppo più lieve che per lo pian non mi parea davanti.
For here, one enters through songs, while below, it is through ferocious laments. We were already climbing the holy steps, and I felt much lighter than I had before on the plain.
Ond’ io: «Maestro, dì, qual cosa greve levata s’è da me, che nulla quasi per me fatica, andando, si riceve?». Rispuose: «Quando i P che son rimasi ancor nel volto tuo presso che stinti, saranno, com’ è l’un, del tutto rasi,
Then I: "Master, tell me, what heavy thing has lifted from me, that I feel almost no fatigue as I walk?"He replied: "When the P's that remain, still barely visible upon your brow, are entirely erased, as one already has been..."
fier li tuoi piè dal buon voler sì vinti, che non pur non fatica sentiranno, ma fia diletto loro esser sù pinti». Allor fec’ io come color che vanno con cosa in capo non da lor saputa, se non che ’ cenni altrui sospecciar fanno;
"Then," he said, "lift your feet so conquered by the good will that they will not only feel no fatigue, but will delight in being thrust upward."Then I did as those who walk with something upon their head they do not know, except that the gestures of others cause suspicion.
per che la mano ad accertar s’aiuta, e cerca e truova e quello officio adempie che non si può fornir per la veduta; e con le dita de la destra scempie trovai pur sei le lettere che ’ncise quel da le chiavi a me sovra le tempie:
Because the hand assists to make certain, and searches, and finds, and fulfills that office which cannot be performed through sight; and with the simple fingers of my right hand, I found six letters incised upon my temples by him of the keys.

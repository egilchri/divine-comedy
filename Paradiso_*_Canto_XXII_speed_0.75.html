Paradiso * Canto XXII

Overwhelmed by a loud cry from the spirits of the Seventh Heaven, Dante turns to Beatrice for reassurance. She directs him to observe the souls, and the brightest among them moves forward to speak. The spirit identifies himself as Saint Benedict of Nursia, founder of the Benedictine Order. He points out other contemplative souls, including Macarius and Romualdus, before lamenting the corruption of his monastic rule on Earth. After speaking, Saint Benedict and the other spirits ascend a golden ladder that reaches up beyond sight. At Beatrice’s signal, Dante is lifted up the ladder with supernatural speed, leaving the Sphere of Saturn. He arrives in the Eighth Heaven, the Sphere of the Fixed Stars, within the constellation of Gemini. Beatrice then instructs Dante to look down upon the universe he has passed through. He sees the seven planetary spheres below him and the Earth, which appears so small from his vantage point that he smiles at its "vile semblance."

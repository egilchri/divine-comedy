Inferno * Canto XV

Dante and Virgil proceed along a raised stone embankment, shielded by steam from a boiling brook against the rain of fire falling on a desert plain. They encounter a group of souls walking on the burning sand. One soul peers up, recognizes Dante, and pulls on his garment. Despite the man’s scorched features, Dante identifies him as his mentor, Brunetto Latini. Brunetto walks alongside them on the sand below, as he cannot stop without incurring a harsher penalty. He asks how Dante came to be there while still alive and prophesies Dante's future political hardships and literary glory. In turn, Dante expresses his deep gratitude for Brunetto's past teachings. Brunetto points out other notable scholars among his companions before seeing another group approaching. He asks Dante to remember his great work, the *Tesoro*, then turns and runs swiftly across the plain.

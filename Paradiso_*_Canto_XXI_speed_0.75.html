Paradiso * Canto XXI

Dante and Beatrice ascend to the seventh heaven, the sphere of Saturn. Beatrice explains that she does not smile because her increased radiance would overwhelm Dante’s mortal senses, just as the celestial music is now silent for the same reason. Dante sees a golden ladder ascending out of sight, with innumerable bright souls moving up and down its rungs. One of these souls descends and stops before them. At Beatrice’s prompting, Dante asks the spirit why it approached and why this sphere is silent. The soul explains it was chosen by divine will, a matter of predestination unknowable even to the blessed. It then identifies itself as Peter Damian, a former hermit and reluctant cardinal. He proceeds to condemn the corruption and luxury of the modern clergy. As he finishes speaking, other spirits descend, gather around him, and let out a cry so powerful that Dante is left stunned and unable to comprehend it.

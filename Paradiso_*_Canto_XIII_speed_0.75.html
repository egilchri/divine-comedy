Paradiso * Canto XIII

In the Heaven of the Sun, Dante observes two concentric circles of twelve lights, the souls of the wise, as they complete a circular dance and a song. After they finish, the soul of St. Thomas Aquinas addresses a specific doubt he perceives in Dante’s mind. Dante is confused by Aquinas’s earlier statement that King Solomon’s wisdom was without equal, as Dante believes Adam and Christ possessed the most perfect wisdom. Aquinas explains that while Adam and Christ were created with the highest perfection of human nature, Solomon's wisdom was of a different, specific kind. He asked God for the practical wisdom necessary to be a sufficient king, not for knowledge of metaphysics or logic. Therefore, Solomon’s wisdom was unparalleled specifically in the category of “kingly prudence.” Aquinas concludes by warning Dante against making hasty and imprecise judgments, urging him to be slow to affirm or deny what he does not fully understand.

Paradiso * Canto VIII

Dante and Beatrice ascend to the third heaven, the sphere of Venus. Dante realizes they have arrived by witnessing Beatrice's increase in beauty. They see numerous bright souls moving in a circular dance. A group of these spirits breaks away and approaches them, singing "Hosanna." One soul addresses Dante directly, offering to pause and answer his questions. With Beatrice’s approval, Dante asks the soul to identify itself. The spirit, glowing more intensely, reveals he is Charles Martel. He explains that had he not died young, he would have been king of territories in Provence, Southern Italy, and Hungary. He then laments the poor governance of his brother, which caused a rebellion in Sicily. Dante then asks how a good parent can produce a bad child. Charles Martel explains that divine providence assigns different natural abilities to different individuals to fulfill the various roles society requires. He concludes that worldly problems arise when people are forced into vocations that are contrary to their innate dispositions.

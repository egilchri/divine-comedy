Purgatorio * Canto IX

As dawn approaches in the Valley of the Rulers, Dante falls asleep. He dreams that a golden eagle seizes him and carries him upward into a sphere of fire, the intense heat of which awakens him. He finds himself in a new location, alone with Virgil. His guide explains that while Dante slept, a lady named Lucia carried him up the mountain to the entrance of Purgatory proper. The two poets approach the gate, which sits atop three steps of different colors. An angel with a sword guards the entrance. After Virgil explains their divine guidance, the angel permits them to ascend. Dante prostrates himself, and the angel carves the letter 'P' seven times on his forehead with the sword. The angel then uses a gold and a silver key to unlock the gate, warning them not to look back. As they enter, Dante hears what sounds like a choir singing the "Te Deum laudamus."
La concubina di Titone antico già s’imbiancava al balco d’orïente, fuor de le braccia del suo dolce amico; di gemme la sua fronte era lucente, poste in figura del freddo animale che con la coda percuote la gente;
Tithonus' ancient concubine was already whitening the eastern balcony, outside the arms of her sweet friend; her brow was shining with gems, arranged in the likeness of the cold beast that strikes people with its tail;
e la notte, de’ passi con che sale, fatti avea due nel loco ov’ eravamo, e ’l terzo già chinava in giuso l’ale; quand’ io, che meco avea di quel d’Adamo, vinto dal sonno, in su l’erba inchinai là ’ve già tutti e cinque sedavamo.
And the night, in the steps by which it ascends, had already taken two where we were, and the third was already lowering its wings; when I, who with me had of that of Adam, overcome by sleep, bent upon the grass there where we five already sat.
Ne l’ora che comincia i tristi lai la rondinella presso a la mattina, forse a memoria de’ suo’ primi guai, e che la mente nostra, peregrina più da la carne e men da’ pensier presa, a le sue visïon quasi è divina,
At the hour the swallow begins its mournful cries near dawn, perhaps in memory of its first sorrows, and when our minds, wandering further from the flesh and less preoccupied with thoughts, are almost divine in their visions,
in sogno mi parea veder sospesa un’aguglia nel ciel con penne d’oro, con l’ali aperte e a calare intesa; ed esser mi parea là dove fuoro abbandonati i suoi da Ganimede, quando fu ratto al sommo consistoro.
In a dream, I seemed to see an eagle hanging in the sky, its feathers gold, wings spread, intent on descending. And I seemed to be where Ganymede’s kin were abandoned when he was snatched away to the highest council.
Fra me pensava: ‘Forse questa fiede pur qui per uso, e forse d’altro loco disdegna di portarne suso in piede’. Poi mi parea che, poi rotata un poco, terribil come folgor discendesse, e me rapisse suso infino al foco.
Within myself I thought: "Perhaps this creature strikes here for a purpose, and perhaps it disdains to carry prey up from another place." Then it seemed to me that, having circled a little, it descended, terrifying as lightning, and snatched me up to the fire.
Ivi parea che ella e io ardesse; e sì lo ’ncendio imaginato cosse, che convenne che ’l sonno si rompesse. Non altrimenti Achille si riscosse, li occhi svegliati rivolgendo in giro e non sappiendo là dove si fosse,
There, both she and I burned; and the imagined fire so seared, that sleep had to break. Not otherwise did Achilles rouse himself, turning his awakened eyes round about, not knowing where he was.
quando la madre da Chirón a Schiro trafuggò lui dormendo in le sue braccia, là onde poi li Greci il dipartiro; che mi scoss’ io, sì come da la faccia mi fuggì ’l sonno, e diventa’ ismorto, come fa l’uom che, spaventato, agghiaccia.
When his mother spirited him away from Chiron to Scyros, sleeping in her arms, there from which the Greeks later removed him, I started as sleep fled from my face, and I grew pale, like a man who, terrified, turns to ice.
Dallato m’era solo il mio conforto, e ’l sole er’ alto già più che due ore, e ’l viso m’era a la marina torto. «Non aver tema», disse il mio segnore; «fatti sicur, ché noi semo a buon punto; non stringer, ma rallarga ogne vigore.
My only comfort had abandoned me, and the sun was already more than two hours high, and my face was turned toward the sea. "Have no fear," said my lord; "be of good cheer, for we are at a good point; do not strain, but relax every strength.
Tu se’ omai al purgatorio giunto: vedi là il balzo che ’l chiude dintorno; vedi l’entrata là ’ve par digiunto. Dianzi, ne l’alba che procede al giorno, quando l’anima tua dentro dormia, sovra li fiori ond’ è là giù addorno
You have now arrived at Purgatory: see there the cliff that encloses it round about; see the entrance there where it appears to be disjoined. Just now, at the dawn that precedes the day, when your soul was sleeping within, above the flowers with which it is adorned below,
venne una donna, e disse: “I’ son Lucia; lasciatemi pigliar costui che dorme; sì l’agevolerò per la sua via”. Sordel rimase e l’altre genti forme; ella ti tolse, e come ’l dì fu chiaro, sen venne suso; e io per le sue orme.
Then came a woman, and she said: “I am Lucia; allow me to take this man who sleeps; thus I will ease him on his way.” Sordello remained, and the other noble forms; she took you, and when the day was clear, she came up here; and I followed in her footsteps.
Qui ti posò, ma pria mi dimostraro li occhi suoi belli quella intrata aperta; poi ella e ’l sonno ad una se n’andaro». A guisa d’uom che ’n dubbio si raccerta e che muta in conforto sua paura, poi che la verità li è discoperta,
She left you there, but first her beautiful eyes showed me that open entrance; then she and sleep went away together." Like a man who reassures himself in doubt and changes his fear to comfort once the truth has been revealed,
mi cambia’ io; e come sanza cura vide me ’l duca mio, su per lo balzo si mosse, e io di rietro inver’ l’altura. Lettor, tu vedi ben com’ io innalzo la mia matera, e però con più arte non ti maravigliar s’io la rincalzo.
I changed then; and as my guide saw me without worry, he moved upward along the cliff, and I followed him back toward the height. Reader, you see well how I raise up my subject, and therefore do not be amazed if I reinforce it with greater skill.
Noi ci appressammo, ed eravamo in parte che là dove pareami prima rotto, pur come un fesso che muro diparte, vidi una porta, e tre gradi di sotto per gire ad essa, di color diversi, e un portier ch’ancor non facea motto.
We drew near, and we were at a point where, at the place that had seemed broken before, just as a fissure divides a wall, I saw a door, and three steps below to ascend to it, of different colors, and a gatekeeper who still had not spoken a word.
E come l’occhio più e più v’apersi, vidil seder sovra ’l grado sovrano, tal ne la faccia ch’io non lo soffersi; e una spada nuda avëa in mano, che reflettëa i raggi sì ver’ noi, ch’io drizzava spesso il viso in vano.
And as my eye opened more and more, I saw him seated upon the highest step, with a face I could not bear; and he had a naked sword in hand, which reflected the light so towards us, that I often turned my face in vain.
«Dite costinci: che volete voi?», cominciò elli a dire, «ov’ è la scorta? Guardate che ’l venir sù non vi nòi».
"Tell me, those of you down there: what do you want?" he began to say. "Where is your guide? Be careful that climbing up doesn't harm you."
«Donna del ciel, di queste cose accorta», rispuose ’l mio maestro a lui, «pur dianzi ne disse: “Andate là: quivi è la porta”». «Ed ella i passi vostri in bene avanzi», ricominciò il cortese portinaio: «Venite dunque a’ nostri gradi innanzi».
"Lady of Heaven, wise in these matters," my master answered him, "told us just now: 'Go there: there is the gate.'" "And may she advance your steps in safety," the courteous gatekeeper began again: "Come then, forward to our stairs."
Là ne venimmo; e lo scaglion primaio bianco marmo era sì pulito e terso, ch’io mi specchiai in esso qual io paio. Era il secondo tinto più che perso, d’una petrina ruvida e arsiccia, crepata per lo lungo e per traverso.
There we arrived; and the first step, made of white marble, was so polished and smooth that I saw myself reflected in it. The second step was more darkly colored than any shade of purple, made from rough and scorched stone, cracked lengthwise and crosswise.
Lo terzo, che di sopra s’ammassiccia, porfido mi parea, sì fiammeggiante come sangue che fuor di vena spiccia. Sovra questo tenëa ambo le piante l’angel di Dio sedendo in su la soglia che mi sembiava pietra di diamante.
The third step, piled up above the others, seemed to me to be porphyry, so fiery, like blood spurting forth from a vein. Upon this, the angel of God kept both feet, sitting on the threshold, which appeared to me to be a stone of diamond.
Per li tre gradi sù di buona voglia mi trasse il duca mio, dicendo: «Chiedi umilemente che ’l serrame scioglia». Divoto mi gittai a’ santi piedi; misericordia chiesi e ch’el m’aprisse, ma tre volte nel petto pria mi diedi.
My guide drew me upward, with good will, over the three steps, saying, “Ask humbly that he unbar the gate.” I cast myself devoutly at his holy feet and begged for mercy, that he might open to me, but first I struck my breast three times.
Sette P ne la fronte mi descrisse col punton de la spada, e «Fa che lavi, quando se’ dentro, queste piaghe» disse. Cenere, o terra che secca si cavi, d’un color fora col suo vestimento; e di sotto da quel trasse due chiavi.
Seven Ps he inscribed on my forehead with the point of his sword, and said, "See that you wash these wounds when you are inside." Ashes, or earth that is dug dry, were the same color as his garment; and from beneath that he drew forth two keys.
L’una era d’oro e l’altra era d’argento; pria con la bianca e poscia con la gialla fece a la porta sì, ch’i’ fu’ contento. «Quandunque l’una d’este chiavi falla, che non si volga dritta per la toppa», diss’ elli a noi, «non s’apre questa calla.
One was gold, and the other silver; first with the white, then with the yellow, he worked on the door, and I was satisfied. "Whenever one of these keys fails, such that it does not turn straight in the lock," he said to us, "this passage does not open."
Più cara è l’una; ma l’altra vuol troppa d’arte e d’ingegno avanti che diserri, perch’ ella è quella che ’l nodo digroppa. Da Pier le tegno; e dissemi ch’i’ erri anzi ad aprir ch’a tenerla serrata, pur che la gente a’ piedi mi s’atterri».
The one is more precious; but the other demandstoo much skill and ingenuity before it unlocks,because it is the one that unravels the knot.I hold them from Peter; and he told me that I errmore by opening than by keeping it shut,provided that the people grovel at my feet.”
Poi pinse l’uscio a la porta sacrata, dicendo: «Intrate; ma facciovi accorti che di fuor torna chi ’n dietro si guata». E quando fuor ne’ cardini distorti li spigoli di quella regge sacra, che di metallo son sonanti e forti,
Then he pushed the door to the sacred portal, saying: "Enter; but I warn you that whoever looks back will return outside." And when the edges of that sacred place, made of resounding and strong metal, were distorted on their hinges,
non rugghiò sì né si mostrò sì acra Tarpëa, come tolto le fu il buono Metello, per che poi rimase macra. Io mi rivolsi attento al primo tuono, e ‘Te Deum laudamus’ mi parea udire in voce mista al dolce suono.
Tarpëa never roared so, nor did she appear so bitter, as when the good Metellus was taken from her, and she was left lean. I turned, attentive to the first thunder, and I seemed to hear “Te Deum laudamus” in a voice mixed with sweet sound.
Tale imagine a punto mi rendea ciò ch’io udiva, qual prender si suole quando a cantar con organi si stea; ch’or sì or no s’intendon le parole.
That song brought to mind the scene,the way one understandswhen singing with organs,the words now clear, now blurred.

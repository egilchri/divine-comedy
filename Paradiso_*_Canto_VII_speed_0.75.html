Paradiso * Canto VII

The canto opens as the soul of Justinian sings "Osanna, sanctus Deus sabaòth." After singing, Justinian's spirit and the other souls in the sphere of Mercury perform a dance and then ascend rapidly, vanishing from Dante's sight. Dante is left with a profound question about divine justice but hesitates to speak to Beatrice out of reverence. Perceiving his unasked question, Beatrice smiles and begins a detailed explanation. She addresses his confusion about how the just punishment of Christ's crucifixion could itself be justly avenged by the destruction of Jerusalem. She explains the dual nature of the Crucifixion and why that specific mode of redemption was chosen. Beatrice then anticipates a second doubt in Dante's mind concerning the mortality of the elements versus the immortality of the human soul, explaining the difference between things created directly by God and those created through secondary causes. This leads to a final point on the certainty of human resurrection.
«Osanna, sanctus Deus sabaòth, superillustrans claritate tua felices ignes horum malacòth!». Così, volgendosi a la nota sua, fu viso a me cantare essa sustanza, sopra la qual doppio lume s’addua;
"Hosanna, holy God of Sabaoth,super-illuminating with your claritythe blessed fires of these mal'achoth!" Thus, turning to its own melody,that essence was seen by me to sing,upon which a double light is cast.
ed essa e l’altre mossero a sua danza, e quasi velocissime faville mi si velar di sùbita distanza. Io dubitava e dicea ‘Dille, dille!’ fra me, ‘dille’ dicea, ‘a la mia donna che mi diseta con le dolci stille’.
And she and the others began their dance, and, like the swiftest sparks, vanished from me in sudden distance. I was doubtful and said to myself, "Tell her, tell her!" I said, "Tell her to my lady, who quenches my thirst with sweet dew."
Ma quella reverenza che s’indonna di tutto me, pur per Be e per ice, mi richinava come l’uom ch’assonna. Poco sofferse me cotal Beatrice e cominciò, raggiandomi d’un riso tal, che nel foco faria l’uom felice:
But that reverence that lords over all of me, just for "Be" and "ice", bent me over like a man who is falling asleep. Beatrice endured me like this for only a moment, and then began, showering me with a smile such that it would make a man happy in a fire:
«Secondo mio infallibile avviso, come giusta vendetta giustamente punita fosse, t’ha in pensier miso; ma io ti solverò tosto la mente; e tu ascolta, ché le mie parole di gran sentenza ti faran presente.
"According to my infallible judgment, how just revenge was justly punished, has set your thoughts awry; but I will swiftly free your mind; and you listen, for my words will present you with a great pronouncement."
Per non soffrire a la virtù che vole freno a suo prode, quell’ uom che non nacque, dannando sé, dannò tutta sua prole; onde l’umana specie inferma giacque giù per secoli molti in grande errore, fin ch’al Verbo di Dio discender piacque
So as not to bridle virtue's desire for the good of that man never born, he damned himself and thus damned all his progeny. Therefore, the human race lay infirm, for many centuries steeped in great error, until the Word of God deigned to descend.
u’ la natura, che dal suo fattore s’era allungata, unì a sé in persona con l’atto sol del suo etterno amore. Or drizza il viso a quel ch’or si ragiona: questa natura al suo fattore unita, qual fu creata, fu sincera e buona;
And nature, which had reached outward from its maker, united itself in one person through the simple act of his eternal love. Now direct your gaze to what is being spoken of: this nature, united with its maker, as it was created, was pure and good;
ma per sé stessa pur fu ella sbandita di paradiso, però che si torse da via di verità e da sua vita. La pena dunque che la croce porse s’a la natura assunta si misura, nulla già mai sì giustamente morse;
But by herself, she was cast out from Paradise because she turned away from the path of truth and from her life. The suffering that the cross offered, measured against the assumed nature, had never bitten so justly.
e così nulla fu di tanta ingiura, guardando a la persona che sofferse, in che era contratta tal natura. Però d’un atto uscir cose diverse: ch’a Dio e a’ Giudei piacque una morte; per lei tremò la terra e ’l ciel s’aperse.
And so no wrong was so great,considering the person who suffered,in whom that nature was bound. Therefore, diverse things came from a single act:for God and the Jews, one death was pleasing;because of it, the earth trembled and the heavens opened.
Non ti dee oramai parer più forte, quando si dice che giusta vendetta poscia vengiata fu da giusta corte. Ma io veggi’ or la tua mente ristretta di pensiero in pensier dentro ad un nodo, del qual con gran disio solver s’aspetta.
You should no longer find it strange,when it is said that just vengeancewas later avenged by a just court. But now I see your mind constrainedby thought upon thought within a knot,which you greatly desire to untie.
Tu dici: “Ben discerno ciò ch’i’ odo; ma perché Dio volesse, m’è occulto, a nostra redenzion pur questo modo”. Questo decreto, frate, sta sepulto a li occhi di ciascuno il cui ingegno ne la fiamma d’amor non è adulto.
You say: “I understand well what I hear, but why God willed it, is hidden from me, this path toward our redemption.” This decree, brother, is buried from the eyes of anyone whose intellect is not mature in love’s flame.
Veramente, però ch’a questo segno molto si mira e poco si discerne, dirò perché tal modo fu più degno. La divina bontà, che da sé sperne ogne livore, ardendo in sé, sfavilla sì che dispiega le bellezze etterne.
Truly, since this sign is much looked at and little understood, I will say why such a way was more worthy. Divine goodness, which casts off all envy, burning within itself, shines forth, so that it unfolds eternal beauties.
Ciò che da lei sanza mezzo distilla non ha poi fine, perché non si move la sua imprenta quand’ ella sigilla. Ciò che da essa sanza mezzo piove libero è tutto, perché non soggiace a la virtute de le cose nove.
What it distills directly from itself knows no end, because its impression does not shift when it seals. Whatever rains down from it directly is completely free, because it does not submit to the power of new things.
Più l’è conforme, e però più le piace; ché l’ardor santo ch’ogne cosa raggia, ne la più somigliante è più vivace. Di tutte queste dote s’avvantaggia l’umana creatura, e s’una manca, di sua nobilità convien che caggia.
The more it conforms, the more it pleases it; for the holy ardor that radiates all things is more lively in the most similar. Of all these gifts, the human creature avails itself, and if one is lacking, it must fall from its nobility.
Solo il peccato è quel che la disfranca e falla dissimìle al sommo bene, per che del lume suo poco s’imbianca; e in sua dignità mai non rivene, se non rïempie, dove colpa vòta, contra mal dilettar con giuste pene.
Only sin is what sets it freeand makes it dissimilar to the highest good,so that it whitens little from its light;and it never returns to its dignity,unless it refills, where guilt empties,against wicked pleasure with just punishments.
Vostra natura, quando peccò tota nel seme suo, da queste dignitadi, come di paradiso, fu remota; né ricovrar potiensi, se tu badi ben sottilmente, per alcuna via, sanza passar per un di questi guadi:
Your nature, when it wholly sinnedin its seed, was removed from these dignities,as from paradise; nor could it be recovered, if you considerclosely, by any means,without passing through one of these fords:
o che Dio solo per sua cortesia dimesso avesse, o che l’uom per sé isso avesse sodisfatto a sua follia. Ficca mo l’occhio per entro l’abisso de l’etterno consiglio, quanto puoi al mio parlar distrettamente fisso.
or that God, alone through His grace, had lowered Himself, or that man, through himself, had satisfied his folly. Now, fix your eye deep within the abyss of the eternal counsel, as closely as you can, focused on my words.
Non potea l’uomo ne’ termini suoi mai sodisfar, per non potere ir giuso con umiltate obedïendo poi, quanto disobediendo intese ir suso; e questa è la cagion per che l’uom fue da poter sodisfar per sé dischiuso.
Man, within his own limits, could never atone, as he was unable to descend in humility and obedience, even after he had sought to ascend in disobedience; and this is the reason why man was shut off from being able to atone on his own.
Dunque a Dio convenia con le vie sue riparar l’omo a sua intera vita, dico con l’una, o ver con amendue. Ma perché l’ovra tanto è più gradita da l’operante, quanto più appresenta de la bontà del core ond’ ell’ è uscita,
Thus it behooved God to repair man to his full life by His own ways, I say with one, or rather, with both. But because the work is so much more pleasing to the one performing it, the more it presents the goodness of the heart from which it issued,
la divina bontà che ’l mondo imprenta, di proceder per tutte le sue vie, a rilevarvi suso, fu contenta. Né tra l’ultima notte e ’l primo die sì alto o sì magnifico processo, o per l’una o per l’altra, fu o fie:
The divine goodness, which imprints the world, was pleased to proceed through all its paths to raise you up. Neither between the last night and the first day was there, nor will there be, such a lofty or magnificent undertaking, whether by one means or another.
ché più largo fu Dio a dar sé stesso per far l’uom sufficiente a rilevarsi, che s’elli avesse sol da sé dimesso; e tutti li altri modi erano scarsi a la giustizia, se ’l Figliuol di Dio non fosse umilïato ad incarnarsi.
For God's generosity was greater in giving Himself to make humanity capable of rising up, than if He had simply forgiven us. And all other means were insufficient to justice, had the Son of God not humbled Himself to become incarnate.
Or per empierti bene ogne disio, ritorno a dichiararti in alcun loco, perché tu veggi lì così com’ io. Tu dici: “Io veggio l’acqua, io veggio il foco, l’aere e la terra e tutte lor misture venire a corruzione, e durar poco;
To fully satisfy your every longing, I will return to explain to you in a certain place, so that you may see there as I do. You say: "I see the water, I see the fire, the air and the earth, and all their mixtures, coming to corruption, and enduring but a little while;
e queste cose pur furon creature; per che, se ciò ch’è detto è stato vero, esser dovrien da corruzion sicure”. Li angeli, frate, e ’l paese sincero nel qual tu se’, dir si posson creati, sì come sono, in loro essere intero;
and these things were also created. Therefore, if what has been said is true, they should be safe from corruption.” Angels, brother, and the pure place in which you are, can be said to have been created, as they are, in their complete being;
ma li alimenti che tu hai nomati e quelle cose che di lor si fanno da creata virtù sono informati. Creata fu la materia ch’elli hanno; creata fu la virtù informante in queste stelle che ’ntorno a lor vanno.
But the foods you have named, and those things that are made of them, are formed by a created power. The matter that they possess was created; the informing power was created in these stars that move around them.
L’anima d’ogne bruto e de le piante di complession potenzïata tira lo raggio e ’l moto de le luci sante; ma vostra vita sanza mezzo spira la somma beninanza, e la innamora di sé sì che poi sempre la disira.
The soul of every beast and of every plant draws its power and movement from the potent combination of holy lights; but your life breathes directly from the highest goodness, which so captivates it that it always desires the source.
E quinci puoi argomentare ancora vostra resurrezion, se tu ripensi come l’umana carne fessi allora che li primi parenti intrambo fensi».
From this you can further infer your resurrection, if you consider how human flesh was made at that time when the first parents were both created.

The world, in its peril, used to believe that fair Cypris, turning in the third epicycle, radiated mad love.

Paradiso * Canto XVI

In the Sphere of Mars, Dante formally addresses his great-great-grandfather, Cacciaguida, prompting a smile from Beatrice. Dante then asks Cacciaguida to identify his ancestors, state his birth year, and describe the population and leading families of 12th-century Florence. Cacciaguida's spirit glows brighter as he responds in an archaic tongue. He states he was born in 1091 in a specific district of Florence but declines to name his own ancestors. He describes the Florence of his era as a small city with a pure, unmixed citizenry, contrasting it with the corrupt, expanded Florence of Dante's time. Cacciaguida proceeds to list numerous noble families who were prominent in his day, noting how many have since declined or disappeared. He identifies the feud involving the Buondelmonti family as the catalyst for the factional strife that later plagued the city, concluding with a memory of a time when Florence was peaceful and just.

Purgatorio * Canto II

At dawn on the shores of Mount Purgatory, Dante and Virgil witness a bright light moving swiftly across the sea. The light resolves into a boat piloted by a luminous angel, who propels the vessel with his wings. Onboard are over one hundred souls singing a psalm. The angel makes the sign of the cross, the souls disembark onto the beach, and the boat immediately departs. The newly arrived spirits, unfamiliar with the location, ask the poets for the way up the mountain. They then notice that Dante is a living being and gather around him in amazement. One shade, whom Dante recognizes as his friend the musician Casella, attempts to embrace him, but Dante’s arms pass through the insubstantial form. At Dante’s request, Casella sings a song, captivating all the souls, as well as Dante and Virgil. Their listening is interrupted by Cato, who sternly rebukes them for the delay and commands them to proceed to the mountain. Startled, the souls scatter and run towards the slope.

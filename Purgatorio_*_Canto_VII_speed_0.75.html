Purgatorio * Canto VII

After initial greetings, the soul Sordello asks Virgil and Dante for their identities. When Virgil reveals his name, Sordello reacts with astonishment and reverence, embracing him. Virgil explains that he comes from Limbo and asks for the path to Purgatory proper. Sordello informs him that night is falling and that the darkness makes it impossible for anyone to ascend the mountain. He clarifies that no external force blocks the way, but the night itself removes the ability to climb higher. Sordello then leads the two poets to a nearby resting place for the night: a beautiful, secluded valley with fragrant air and brilliantly colored grass and flowers. In this valley, they see a group of souls singing the hymn 'Salve, Regina'. From a ledge overlooking the area, Sordello points out and identifies several of these figures to Dante, naming them as various European kings and princes who were negligent in their earthly duties.

Purgatorio * Canto XXVIII

Dante enters a beautiful forest at the summit of Mount Purgatory. He walks through the woods until his path is blocked by a clear stream. On the opposite bank, he sees a solitary woman, singing and gathering flowers from the meadow. Dante calls out, asking her to move closer to the bank so he can understand her song. She approaches, smiling, separated from him only by the water. Dante questions her about the presence of the stream and the wind, which he believed did not exist at this altitude. The woman explains that the "wind" is the constant motion of the celestial spheres and that the stream flows from a divine, unwavering source. She identifies the river's two branches: Lethe, which removes the memory of sin, and Eunoè, which restores the memory of good deeds. She concludes by stating this place is the Earthly Paradise, which ancient poets wrote of as the Golden Age. Dante then turns and sees Virgil and Statius smiling.

And she said, "I will explain how the thing that causes your wonder proceeds, and I will clear away the fog that troubles you. The highest Good, who alone finds joy in Himself, made man good and for good, and gave him this place as a pledge of eternal peace."

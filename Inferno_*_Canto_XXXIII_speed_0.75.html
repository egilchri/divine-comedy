Inferno * Canto XXXIII

In the ninth circle of Hell, Dante and Virgil witness Count Ugolino gnawing on the head of Archbishop Ruggieri. At Dante’s request, Ugolino pauses to recount his death. He explains how Ruggieri imprisoned him and his four sons in a tower, sealing the entrance and starving them. Ugolino describes watching each of his sons die over the course of several days. After they were all dead, he, having gone blind, stumbled over their bodies for two days before he also succumbed to starvation. He then resumes his savage meal. The poets proceed into Ptolomea, the third ring of Cocytus, where traitors to guests are frozen supine in the ice. Their tears freeze in their eye sockets, sealing their eyes. One soul, Fra Alberigo, begs Dante to remove the ice. He explains that souls guilty of this sin can fall to Hell before their bodies die, while a demon possesses their physical form on Earth. He points out Branca Doria, whom Dante knows to be still alive. Dante refuses to help Alberigo and leaves.

Purgatorio * Canto VI

Dante is immediately surrounded by a crowd of souls who died by violence and repented at the last moment. They press in on him, begging for prayers to shorten their time in Ante-Purgatory. Dante names several, including Benincasa da Laterina, Federigo Novello, and Pierre de la Broce. After extricating himself from the crowd with promises, Dante asks Virgil how prayers from the living can alter divine judgment. Virgil offers a brief explanation, stating that Beatrice will provide a fuller answer at the top of the mountain. As they proceed, Virgil spots a solitary soul sitting apart who might show them the quickest path. They approach the soul, who remains aloof. When Virgil asks for directions, the soul instead asks for their origin. Upon hearing Virgil begin to say “Mantua,” the soul rises, reveals he is the troubadour Sordello, and embraces Virgil as a fellow Mantuan.

Paradiso * Canto XXVI

Temporarily blinded by the light of St. John the Evangelist, Dante is prompted by the apostle to explain the object of his love and his reasons for it. After Dante provides his answers, the spirits in the sphere sing a hymn of praise. Beatrice then looks at Dante, and the power of her gaze restores his sight, making it clearer than before. Dante immediately perceives a fourth bright flame among them. Beatrice identifies this soul as Adam. Dante addresses the first man, who, already aware of the pilgrim’s unasked questions, proceeds to answer them. Adam recounts the duration of his stay in the Garden of Eden, the specific nature of his transgression, the original language he spoke, his lifespan on Earth, and the number of years he spent in Limbo before his salvation.

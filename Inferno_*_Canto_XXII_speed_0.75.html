Inferno * Canto XXII

Dante and Virgil continue along the bank of the boiling pitch, escorted by ten demons. They observe the sinners (barrators) who occasionally surface for relief, only to dive back down when the demons approach. The demon Graffiacane hooks one sinner by his hair and pulls him out. At Virgil’s prompting, the sinner identifies himself as a man from Navarre who practiced graft. He also names two other sinners submerged nearby, Fra Gomita and Michel Zanche. As the demons torment him, the Navarrese sinner proposes a trick: if the demons stand back, he will whistle to lure more sinners to the surface. One demon, Alichino, accepts the challenge. The sinner then seizes the opportunity to dive back into the pitch and escape. Enraged by the deception, Alichino is attacked by another demon, Calcabrina. The two fight in mid-air and fall into the pitch, where their wings become stuck. As their leader sends four demons to rescue them, Dante and Virgil leave the scene.

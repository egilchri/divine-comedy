Purgatorio * Canto IV

Dante and Virgil begin the difficult ascent of the mountain through a narrow, steep fissure in the rock, requiring the use of both hands and feet. After a strenuous effort, Dante becomes exhausted, and Virgil leads him to a rocky ledge to rest. Sitting down, Dante is perplexed to see the sun on their left (in the north). Virgil provides a detailed astronomical explanation for why the sun's path appears different in the Southern Hemisphere. Their discussion is interrupted by a voice from a group of souls resting in the shade of a large boulder. These are the late-repentant who were indolent. Dante recognizes one, Belacqua, who is sitting with his head between his knees. Dante speaks with him, and Belacqua explains that he must wait in Ante-Purgatory for a period equal to his lifetime before beginning his penance. As they finish, Virgil notes that it is midday and urges Dante to continue climbing.

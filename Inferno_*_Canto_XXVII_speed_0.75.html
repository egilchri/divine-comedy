Inferno * Canto XXVII

As Dante and Virgil depart from the flame of Ulysses, another flame approaches them, emitting a garbled sound. The soul within, having recognized Dante’s Lombard dialect, asks for news about the political state of Romagna. Virgil instructs Dante to answer. Dante provides a detailed report on the region's tyrannical rulers and ongoing conflicts, then asks the soul to reveal its identity. The spirit identifies himself as Guido da Montefeltro. He explains that he was a soldier who became a Franciscan friar but was drawn back into sin when Pope Boniface VIII requested his fraudulent counsel for a military campaign. The Pope offered pre-emptive absolution for the sin. After Guido’s death, a demon claimed his soul, arguing that one cannot be absolved for a sin they intend to commit. Minos condemned him to this chasm. After concluding his story, the flame writhes in sorrow and departs, and the poets continue their journey.

Paradiso * Canto III

In the Sphere of the Moon, Dante sees faint images of faces, which he mistakes for reflections. Beatrice corrects him, explaining they are the actual souls of those who were inconstant in their vows. Dante speaks to one spirit, who identifies herself as Piccarda Donati. She recounts that she was a nun forcibly removed from her convent. Dante asks if the souls in this lowest sphere desire a higher place in Heaven. Piccarda explains that their wills are perfectly aligned with God's will, and therefore they are completely content with their placement. She identifies another soul, Empress Constance, who also was forced from her cloister. After speaking, Piccarda sings "Ave, Maria" and fades from view. Dante turns back to Beatrice, but her radiance is so overwhelming that he is unable to look at her or to speak further.

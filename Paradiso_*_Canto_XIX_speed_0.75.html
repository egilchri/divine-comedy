Paradiso * Canto XIX

In the sixth heaven, the sphere of Jupiter, Dante observes the souls of the just rulers arrange themselves into the shape of a giant, luminous eagle. The eagle speaks with a single voice, representing the unified will of the souls. It states that it is rewarded in this heaven for having been just and pious on Earth. Dante, addressing the eagle, asks it to resolve a profound doubt: how is it just for a virtuous person, who lived and died without ever hearing of Christ, to be condemned? The eagle responds first by stating that human intellect is too limited to fully comprehend divine justice. It then directly addresses the question, declaring that no one has ever entered heaven without faith in Christ, either before or after his crucifixion. The eagle concludes by delivering a long and detailed condemnation of the unjust Christian kings ruling Europe in Dante's time, listing their specific sins and predicting their future judgment.

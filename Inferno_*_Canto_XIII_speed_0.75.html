Inferno * Canto XIII

After crossing the river Phlegethon, Dante and Virgil enter a bleak, tangled wood in the second ring of the seventh circle. The trees are dark and gnarled, and perched among them are Harpies, creatures with the faces of women and the bodies of birds, who emit mournful cries. Dante hears lamentations but sees no one, so Virgil instructs him to break a branch from a tree. When Dante snaps a small twig, the tree cries out in pain and bleeds from the wound. The soul trapped within identifies himself as Pier della Vigna, a former advisor to Emperor Frederick II. He explains that souls who died by suicide are transformed into these trees and are perpetually tormented by the Harpies feeding on their leaves. Their conversation is interrupted by two naked souls running through the woods, chased by a pack of ferocious black hounds. The dogs catch one of the men, Jacopo da Sant' Andrea, and tear him to pieces. Dante and Virgil then approach the bush that was damaged in the chase, and its soul, a Florentine, speaks to them.

Purgatorio * Canto XXIII

While walking on the sixth terrace, Dante, Virgil, and Statius hear a group of shades weeping and singing. These souls are extremely emaciated, with pale faces and hollow eyes. One of the shades recognizes Dante by his voice, and Dante, in turn, identifies the speaker as his friend, Forese Donati. Forese explains that the souls on this terrace are being cleansed of gluttony. Their hunger and thirst are perpetually stimulated by the scent from a fruit tree and the spray from a spring of water, both of which are inaccessible to them. Dante expresses surprise at finding Forese so high on the mountain, since he died less than five years prior. Forese reveals that his rapid progress is due to the constant, devout prayers of his wife, Nella. After Forese points out that the other shades are staring at the living Dante, he asks for an explanation. Dante briefly recounts his journey from the dark wood and identifies his guides, Virgil and Statius.

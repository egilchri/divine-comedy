Purgatorio * Canto X

After passing through the Gate of Purgatory, Dante and Virgil ascend a narrow, winding path. They emerge onto a flat, deserted terrace, the first cornice of the mountain. The inner cliff face is made of white marble, intricately sculpted with lifelike reliefs. Dante observes three specific scenes carved into the rock wall. The first depicts the Archangel Gabriel's Annunciation to the Virgin Mary. The second shows King David dancing before the Ark of the Covenant, with his wife Michal looking on from a palace window. The third carving illustrates the Emperor Trajan on horseback, stopped by a widow who pleads for justice for her son. As Dante examines these images, Virgil points out a group of souls approaching. They move slowly because they are bent over, each carrying a massive stone on their back. The heavy burden crushes their forms, forcing their knees toward their chests, and they are heard weeping that they can endure no more.

Inferno * Canto XII

Dante and Virgil descend a treacherous, rocky slope into the Seventh Circle. At the precipice, they encounter the Minotaur. Virgil taunts the beast, creating a diversion that allows them to hurry past its rage. As they proceed, Virgil explains that the shattered rock was caused by the great earthquake that occurred when Christ descended into Hell. They arrive at the Phlegethon, a wide river of boiling blood. Along its banks, armed centaurs patrol, firing arrows at the souls of tyrants and murderers who attempt to rise out of the blood. Their leader, Chiron, questions the travelers but is answered by Virgil, who explains their divine mission and requests a guide. Chiron notices Dante is alive, as he moves the stones beneath his feet. He instructs the centaur Nessus to lead the poets along the riverbank to a shallow crossing. As they walk, Nessus points out specific sinners submerged to depths corresponding to their crimes.

What ruin is that, which on this side of Trento, the Adige struck at its flank, whether by earthquake or by lack of support? From the mountain's peak, where it began, the rock has so descended to the plain that it would offer no path to anyone who were atop it.

I saw a wide ditch, curved like a bow, that spanned the entire plain, as my guide had said. And between the base of the bank and the ditch, centaurs ran, armed with arrows, as they used to hunt in the world.

Around the ditch they go by the thousands, shooting any soul that pulls itself free from the blood more than its guilt assigned. We approached those swift beasts. Chiron took an arrow, and with the notch, he parted his beard back over his jaws.
Quando s’ebbe scoperta la gran bocca, disse a’ compagni: «Siete voi accorti che quel di retro move ciò ch’el tocca? Così non soglion far li piè d’i morti».
When he had uncovered the great maw, he said to his companions: "Have you noticed that the one behind moves whatever he touches? The feet of the dead are not wont to do so."

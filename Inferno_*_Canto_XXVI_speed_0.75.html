Inferno * Canto XXVI

Dante and Virgil climb from the bolgia of the thieves to a bridge overlooking the eighth bolgia of the eighth circle. From this vantage point, Dante observes the chasm is filled with countless individual flames, each containing a sinner. Virgil confirms this observation. Dante's attention is captured by a single flame that is split into two horns. He asks his guide who is within it. Virgil identifies the spirits as Ulysses and Diomedes, punished together. Dante expresses a strong desire to speak with them, and Virgil agrees to address the flame on his behalf. As the flame approaches, Virgil asks one of the spirits to recount the story of his final journey. The larger horn of the flame begins to speak. It is Ulysses, who describes sailing with his aged crew beyond the Pillars of Hercules into the unknown ocean. After five months, they sighted a massive mountain. A sudden whirlwind struck their ship, spinning it three times before the sea closed over them, drowning all aboard.

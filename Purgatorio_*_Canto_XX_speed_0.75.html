Purgatorio * Canto XX

Dante and Virgil continue along the fifth terrace, where the avaricious lie prostrate. Dante overhears a soul praising the poverty of the Virgin Mary and the Roman consul Fabricius. He approaches and speaks with the spirit, who identifies himself as Hugh Capet, founder of the French monarchy. Capet recounts his family's rise to power and condemns the subsequent avaricious crimes of his descendants. He explains the penitents’ task: during the day, they recite examples of poverty and generosity; at night, they cry out examples of greed. After Dante and Virgil leave the soul, the entire mountain of Purgatory begins to tremble violently, filling Dante with fear. Immediately following the tremor, a unified shout of "Gloria in excelsis Deo" erupts from all the souls on the mountain. Once the shaking and the cry have ceased, the two poets resume their journey, leaving Dante with unspoken questions about the phenomenon.

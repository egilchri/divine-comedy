Purgatorio * Canto XXXI

Across the river Lethe, Beatrice demands that Dante confess his failings. Overcome with shame, Dante whispers his assent and collapses in tears, admitting that after her death, he was led astray by earthly pleasures. Beatrice rebukes him. The sight of her face, now unveiled, fills Dante with such remorse that he faints. He awakens to find the woman he met earlier pulling him into the river. She immerses him, and he swallows some of the water. On the opposite bank, four women receive him, dancing around him and covering him with their arms. They lead him to the foot of the griffin, positioning him directly in front of Beatrice. Staring into her eyes, Dante sees the reflection of the two-natured griffin shifting form. Three other women then arrive, singing and asking Beatrice to reveal her smile to Dante.

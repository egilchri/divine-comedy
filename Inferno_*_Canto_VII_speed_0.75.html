Inferno * Canto VII

At the entrance to the Fourth Circle, Dante and Virgil are confronted by its guardian, Plutus. Virgil silences the creature with a command, and it collapses, allowing the poets to descend. Here they observe two groups of souls pushing enormous weights in a semicircle. The groups collide violently, shouting accusations at each other before turning around to repeat the arduous process. Virgil identifies many as avaricious clergy, but their features are now indiscernible. The travelers cross the circle and arrive at the bank of a dark, boiling spring that flows into the marsh of Styx, which constitutes the Fifth Circle. In the murky water, they see the naked souls of the Wrathful, who are mired in mud and furiously strike and bite one another. Virgil points out that beneath the surface, the Sullen are completely submerged, their unheard laments causing bubbles to rise. The poets continue along the edge of the bog until they reach the foot of a tall tower.

I saw a crowd, more numerous than anywhere else, and they were shouting, some from one side, some from the other, as they strained to roll weights with their chests. Thus they returned throughout the shadowed circle, from either hand to the opposite point, shouting in turn their shameful measure; then each of them, when they had arrived, turned back, traversing the half-circle to the other contest.

And I, whose heart was nearly pricked with shame, said: "My master, now show me who these people are, and whether all those with tonsures on our left were clerics."

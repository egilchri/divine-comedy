Paradiso * Canto XXXIII

The canto opens with Saint Bernard offering a prayer to the Virgin Mary. He asks her to grant the narrator, Dante, the grace to lift his eyes to the final vision of God and to preserve his mortal faculties afterward. Beatrice and other blessed souls join in the prayer. The Virgin Mary looks at Bernard and then turns her gaze toward the "eternal light," signifying her assent. Dante then looks into the light, and his sight becomes progressively stronger, allowing him to see more deeply. Within the light, he perceives the substance, accidents, and principles of the entire universe bound together in a single volume. As he continues to stare, the image transforms into three circles of three different colors occupying the same space. He then sees a human likeness within one of the circles. While his intellect fails to grasp this, his mind is struck by a flash of understanding, and his desire and will are aligned with the power that moves the universe.
tu se’ colei che l’umana natura nobilitasti sì, che ’l suo fattore non disdegnò di farsi sua fattura. Nel ventre tuo si raccese l’amore, per lo cui caldo ne l’etterna pace così è germinato questo fiore.
You are she who so ennobled human nature that its maker did not disdain to be made by it. Within your womb, love was rekindled, and by its warmth, in eternal peace, this flower has sprouted.
Qui se’ a noi meridïana face di caritate, e giuso, intra ’ mortali, se’ di speranza fontana vivace. Donna, se’ tanto grande e tanto vali, che qual vuol grazia e a te non ricorre, sua disïanza vuol volar sanz’ ali.
You are for us a midday sun of charity, and, here below, among mortals, a lively fountain of hope. Lady, you are so great and so worthy that whoever wants grace and does not turn to you, his desire wishes to fly without wings.
La tua benignità non pur soccorre a chi domanda, ma molte fïate liberamente al dimandar precorre. In te misericordia, in te pietate, in te magnificenza, in te s’aduna quantunque in creatura è di bontate.
Your kindness not only aids those who ask, but often freely anticipates the asking. In you, mercy, in you, pity, in you, magnificence, in you, gathers all goodness found in a creature.
Or questi, che da l’infima lacuna de l’universo infin qui ha vedute le vite spiritali ad una ad una, supplica a te, per grazia, di virtute tanto, che possa con li occhi levarsi più alto verso l’ultima salute.
And now, he who from the lowest depthsof the universe has seen, one by one,the lives of spirits, begs of you, as a grace,for such virtue, that with his eyes he may risehigher toward the ultimate salvation.
E io, che mai per mio veder non arsi più ch’i’ fo per lo suo, tutti miei prieghi ti porgo, e priego che non sieno scarsi, perché tu ogne nube li disleghi di sua mortalità co’ prieghi tuoi, sì che ’l sommo piacer li si dispieghi.
And I, who never burned with desire for my own vision more than I do for his, offer you all my prayers, and I pray that they be not lacking, so that with your prayers you may dispel every cloud of his mortality, so that the ultimate joy may be revealed to him.
Ancor ti priego, regina, che puoi ciò che tu vuoli, che conservi sani, dopo tanto veder, li affetti suoi. Vinca tua guardia i movimenti umani: vedi Beatrice con quanti beati per li miei prieghi ti chiudon le mani!».
I beg you again, Queen, that you, who can do as you wish, preserve his affections, sound and whole, after he has seen so much. Let your protection conquer human feelings. See Beatrice, with how many blessed ones, closes her hands to you for my prayers!
Li occhi da Dio diletti e venerati, fissi ne l’orator, ne dimostraro quanto i devoti prieghi le son grati; indi a l’etterno lume s’addrizzaro, nel qual non si dee creder che s’invii per creatura l’occhio tanto chiaro.
The eyes beloved and revered by God, fixed upon the speaker, showed how much the devout prayers pleased her. Then, they turned toward the eternal light, where it should not be believed that any creature could send eyes so bright.
E io ch’al fine di tutt’ i disii appropinquava, sì com’ io dovea, l’ardor del desiderio in me finii. Bernardo m’accennava, e sorridea, perch’ io guardassi suso; ma io era già per me stesso tal qual ei volea:
And I, who neared the end of all desires, as was my due, I brought the ardor of my longing to its close. Bernard gestured to me, and smiled, so that I should look upward; but I was already, of myself, as he desired:
ché la mia vista, venendo sincera, e più e più intrava per lo raggio de l’alta luce che da sé è vera. Da quinci innanzi il mio veder fu maggio che ’l parlar mostra, ch’a tal vista cede, e cede la memoria a tanto oltraggio.
For my sight, becoming pure, entered more and more into the ray of the high light that is true in itself. From this point forward, my vision was greater than speech can show, which fails at such a sight, and memory fails at such excess.
Qual è colüi che sognando vede, che dopo ’l sogno la passione impressa rimane, e l’altro a la mente non riede, cotal son io, ché quasi tutta cessa mia visïone, e ancor mi distilla nel core il dolce che nacque da essa.
Like someone who dreams and sees, and after the dream the passion remains impressed, but the rest does not return to the mind, such am I, for almost all of my vision ceases, and still there distills within my heart the sweetness that was born from it.
Così la neve al sol si disigilla; così al vento ne le foglie levi si perdea la sentenza di Sibilla. O somma luce che tanto ti levi da’ concetti mortali, a la mia mente ripresta un poco di quel che parevi,
Thus the snow unseals in the sun;thus in the light leaves to the windwas lost the Sibyl's pronouncement.O highest light, who so far removedfrom mortal concepts, restore to my minda little of what you appeared to be,
e fa la lingua mia tanto possente, ch’una favilla sol de la tua gloria possa lasciare a la futura gente; ché, per tornare alquanto a mia memoria e per sonare un poco in questi versi, più si conceperà di tua vittoria.
And make my tongue so potentthat a single spark of your gloryit may leave for future people;so that, to return somewhat to my memoryand to sound a little in these verses,more of your victory will be conceived.
Io credo, per l’acume ch’io soffersi del vivo raggio, ch’i’ sarei smarrito, se li occhi miei da lui fossero aversi. E’ mi ricorda ch’io fui più ardito per questo a sostener, tanto ch’i’ giunsi l’aspetto mio col valore infinito.
I believe, because of the sharpness I enduredfrom the living ray, that I would have been lostif my eyes had been turned away from it.And I remember that I was bolderbecause of this, to endure, until I joinedmy vision with the infinite power.
Oh abbondante grazia ond’ io presunsi ficcar lo viso per la luce etterna, tanto che la veduta vi consunsi! Nel suo profondo vidi che s’interna, legato con amore in un volume, ciò che per l’universo si squaderna:
Oh abundant grace, by which I daredto fix my gaze upon the eternal light,until I consumed my sight within it!In its depths, I saw that which is bound,by love, in a single volume,that which is scattered throughout the universe:
sustanze e accidenti e lor costume quasi conflati insieme, per tal modo che ciò ch’i’ dico è un semplice lume. La forma universal di questo nodo credo ch’i’ vidi, perché più di largo, dicendo questo, mi sento ch’i’ godo.
Substance and accidents and their manner,almost fused together, in such a waythat what I say is a simple light.The universal form of this knotI believe I saw, because, in saying this,I feel that I rejoice.
Un punto solo m’è maggior letargo che venticinque secoli a la ’mpresa che fé Nettuno ammirar l’ombra d’Argo. Così la mente mia, tutta sospesa, mirava fissa, immobile e attenta, e sempre di mirar faceasi accesa.
A single point was a greater slumber for me than twenty-five centuries of the undertaking that made Neptune marvel at the shadow of the Argo. Thus my mind, entirely suspended, gazed fixedly, motionless and attentive, and was ever inflamed by the act of gazing.
A quella luce cotal si diventa, che volgersi da lei per altro aspetto è impossibil che mai si consenta; però che ’l ben, ch’è del volere obietto, tutto s’accoglie in lei, e fuor di quella è defettivo ciò ch’è lì perfetto.
In that light, one becomes so transformed that turning away to any other sight is impossible to consent to. Because the good, which is the object of the will, is entirely received in it, and outside of it, that which is perfect there is defective.
Omai sarà più corta mia favella, pur a quel ch’io ricordo, che d’un fante che bagni ancor la lingua a la mammella. Non perché più ch’un semplice sembiante fosse nel vivo lume ch’io mirava, che tal è sempre qual s’era davante;
My speech will be shorter now, only a child's, still wet from his mother's milk. Not because the living light I gazed upon contained more than a single form, which remains as it always was.
ma per la vista che s’avvalorava in me guardando, una sola parvenza, mutandom’ io, a me si travagliava. Ne la profonda e chiara sussistenza de l’alto lume parvermi tre giri di tre colori e d’una contenenza;
but as my sight, gazing upon it, grew stronger, a single appearance, though I changed, was struggling within me. In the deep and clear essence of the high light, three circles appeared to me, of three colors and the same dimension.
e l’un da l’altro come iri da iri parea reflesso, e ’l terzo parea foco che quinci e quindi igualmente si spiri. Oh quanto è corto il dire e come fioco al mio concetto!
And one reflected the other, like rainbows mirroring rainbows, and the third appeared like fire that equally emanated from both sides. Oh, how insufficient is language, and how feeble compared to my conception!
e questo, a quel ch’i’ vidi, è tanto, che non basta a dicer ‘poco’. O luce etterna che sola in te sidi, sola t’intendi, e da te intelletta e intendente te ami e arridi!
And this, from what I saw, is so immense that "little" is not sufficient to describe it. O eternal light, who dwells alone within yourself, you alone understand yourself, and understanding yourself, you love and smile upon yourself!
Quella circulazion che sì concetta pareva in te come lume reflesso, da li occhi miei alquanto circunspetta, dentro da sé, del suo colore stesso, mi parve pinta de la nostra effige: per che ’l mio viso in lei tutto era messo.
That circulation, so conceived, appearing in you like reflected light, observed somewhat by my eyes, within itself, of its own color, seemed to me to be painted with our image; because of which my vision was entirely fixed upon it.
Qual è ’l geomètra che tutto s’affige per misurar lo cerchio, e non ritrova, pensando, quel principio ond’ elli indige, tal era io a quella vista nova: veder voleva come si convenne l’imago al cerchio e come vi s’indova;
Like a geometer who applies himself entirely to measuring the circle, and cannot discover, though thinking, the principle he needs, so was I at that new sight: I wanted to see how the image fitted the circle and how it found its place there;
ma non eran da ciò le proprie penne: se non che la mia mente fu percossa da un fulgore in che sua voglia venne. A l’alta fantasia qui mancò possa; ma già volgeva il mio disio e ’l velle, sì come rota ch’igualmente è mossa,
but my own wings were not fit for that task, save that my mind was struck by a flash wherein its wish came. Here power failed the high imagination; but already my desire and will were turning, as a wheel that is evenly moved,
l’amor che move il sole e l’altre stelle. - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
the love that moves the sun and the other stars.

Inferno * Canto III

Dante and his guide, Virgil, arrive at the Gate of Hell and read its inscription, which ends with the words, "Abandon all hope, you who enter." Inside, in a dark vestibule, they encounter the souls of the Uncommitted. These shades chase a blank banner and are perpetually stung by wasps and hornets; their blood and tears are consumed by worms at their feet. Virgil points out one figure among them and urges Dante to move on. They proceed to the bank of the river Acheron, where they find a crowd of souls waiting to cross. The ferryman, Charon, appears in a boat. He initially denies Dante passage because he is a living soul, but Virgil silences him, stating the journey is willed by a higher authority. Charon then herds the weeping, damned souls into his vessel. As Dante stands on the shore, the ground violently trembles, a red light flashes, and he faints.
‘Per me si va ne la città dolente, per me si va ne l’etterno dolore, per me si va tra la perduta gente. Giustizia mosse il mio alto fattore; fecemi la divina podestate, la somma sapïenza e ’l primo amore.
Through me the way into the grieving city,through me the way into eternal pain,through me the way among the lost people.Justice moved my high maker;divine power made me,the highest wisdom, and the primal love.
Dinanzi a me non fuor cose create se non etterne, e io etterno duro. Lasciate ogne speranza, voi ch’intrate’.
Before me, nothing was created save eternal things, and I endure eternally. Abandon all hope, you who enter.
Queste parole di colore oscuro vid’ ïo scritte al sommo d’una porta; per ch’io: «Maestro, il senso lor m’è duro». Ed elli a me, come persona accorta: «Qui si convien lasciare ogne sospetto; ogne viltà convien che qui sia morta.
These words of somber hueI saw inscribed above a portal,whereupon I: “Master, their meaning is obscure.”And he to me, like one well-schooled:“Here all suspicion must be left behind;here all cowardice must die.”
Noi siam venuti al loco ov’ i’ t’ho detto che tu vedrai le genti dolorose c’hanno perduto il ben de l’intelletto». E poi che la sua mano a la mia puose con lieto volto, ond’ io mi confortai, mi mise dentro a le segrete cose.
“We have arrived at the place where I told youthat you would see the sorrowful peoplewho have lost the good of the intellect.”And then, when he placed his hand on minewith a joyful face, by which I was comforted,he led me into the secret things.
Quivi sospiri, pianti e alti guai risonavan per l’aere sanza stelle, per ch’io al cominciar ne lagrimai. Diverse lingue, orribili favelle, parole di dolore, accenti d’ira, voci alte e fioche, e suon di man con elle
Here, sighs, laments, and loud wails echoed through the starless air, so that I began to weep. Diverse tongues, horrible utterances, words of pain, accents of anger, loud and faint voices, and the sound of hands accompanying them.
facevano un tumulto, il qual s’aggira sempre in quell’ aura sanza tempo tinta, come la rena quando turbo spira. E io ch’avea d’error la testa cinta, dissi: «Maestro, che è quel ch’i’ odo?
They made a tumult that swirls forever in the timeless, shadowed air, like sand when a whirlwind blows. And I, whose head was bound with error, said: "Master, what is this that I hear?"
e che gent’ è che par nel duol sì vinta?». Ed elli a me: «Questo misero modo tegnon l’anime triste di coloro che visser sanza ’nfamia e sanza lodo.
"And who are those people that seem so overcome with grief?" And he to me: "These are the wretched souls of those who lived without infamy and without praise."
Mischiate sono a quel cattivo coro de li angeli che non furon ribelli né fur fedeli a Dio, ma per sé fuoro. Caccianli i ciel per non esser men belli, né lo profondo inferno li riceve, ch’alcuna gloria i rei avrebber d’elli».
They are mixed with that dismal choir of angels who were not rebellious, nor were they faithful to God, but were for themselves. Heaven casts them out, so as not to be less beautiful, nor does the deep Inferno receive them, for the wicked would have some glory from them.”
E io: «Maestro, che è tanto greve a lor che lamentar li fa sì forte?». Rispuose: «Dicerolti molto breve.
And I: “Master, what is so heavy upon them that makes them lament so loudly?” He answered: “I shall tell you very briefly.”
Questi non hanno speranza di morte, e la lor cieca vita è tanto bassa, che ’nvidïosi son d’ogne altra sorte. Fama di loro il mondo esser non lassa; misericordia e giustizia li sdegna: non ragioniam di lor, ma guarda e passa».
These have no hope of death, and their blind life is so wretched that they envy every other fate. The world does not allow a name for them; mercy and justice disdain them: let us not speak of them, but look and pass on.”
E io, che riguardai, vidi una ’nsegna che girando correva tanto ratta, che d’ogne posa mi parea indegna; e dietro le venìa sì lunga tratta di gente, ch’i’ non averei creduto che morte tanta n’avesse disfatta.
And I, who looked on, saw a banner that, circling, ran so fast it seemed unworthy of any rest; and behind it came such a long line of people that I would not have believed death had undone so many.
Poscia ch’io v’ebbi alcun riconosciuto, vidi e conobbi l’ombra di colui che fece per viltade il gran rifiuto. Incontanente intesi e certo fui che questa era la setta d’i cattivi, a Dio spiacenti e a’ nemici sui.
After I had recognized some, I saw and knew the shade of the one who made the great refusal out of cowardice. Immediately I understood and was certain that this was the wretched sect, displeasing to God and to His enemies.
Questi sciaurati, che mai non fur vivi, erano ignudi e stimolati molto da mosconi e da vespe ch’eran ivi. Elle rigavan lor di sangue il volto, che, mischiato di lagrime, a’ lor piedi da fastidiosi vermi era ricolto.
These miserable souls, who never truly lived, were naked and constantly tormented by flies and wasps that swarmed there. These insects stung their faces, drawing blood and tears that, mixing together, flowed down to their feet and were gathered by vile worms.
E poi ch’a riguardar oltre mi diedi, vidi genti a la riva d’un gran fiume; per ch’io dissi: «Maestro, or mi concedi ch’i’ sappia quali sono, e qual costume le fa di trapassar parer sì pronte, com’ i’ discerno per lo fioco lume».
And then, when I turned my gaze further on, I saw a multitude on the banks of a great river; I said: "Master, now grant that I know who they are, and what custom makes them so eager to cross, as I discern in the dim light."
Ed elli a me: «Le cose ti fier conte quando noi fermerem li nostri passi su la trista riviera d’Acheronte». Allor con li occhi vergognosi e bassi, temendo no ’l mio dir li fosse grave, infino al fiume del parlar mi trassi.
And he said to me: "These things will be clear to youwhen we halt our stepsupon the wretched river Acheron." Then, with shamefaced eyes lowered,fearing that my words might be a burden to him,I spoke no more until we reached the river.
Ed ecco verso noi venir per nave un vecchio, bianco per antico pelo, gridando: «Guai a voi, anime prave! Non isperate mai veder lo cielo: i’ vegno per menarvi a l’altra riva ne le tenebre etterne, in caldo e ’n gelo.
And behold, toward us in a boat came an old man, white with ancient hair, crying, "Woe to you, wicked souls! Never hope to see Heaven: I come to bring you to the other shore, into the eternal darkness, in heat and in cold."
E tu che se’ costì, anima viva, pàrtiti da cotesti che son morti». Ma poi che vide ch’io non mi partiva,
"And you, who are there, living soul, depart from these who are dead." But when he saw that I did not depart,
disse: «Per altra via, per altri porti verrai a piaggia, non qui, per passare: più lieve legno convien che ti porti». E ’l duca lui: «Caron, non ti crucciare: vuolsi così colà dove si puote ciò che si vuole, e più non dimandare».
He said: "By another route, by other ferries,you shall arrive at the shore, not here, for passage:a lighter craft must carry you." And the guide to him: "Charon, do not be vexed:it is thus willed where that which is willed can be done,and ask no more."
Quinci fuor quete le lanose gote al nocchier de la livida palude, che ’ntorno a li occhi avea di fiamme rote. Ma quell’ anime, ch’eran lasse e nude, cangiar colore e dibattero i denti, ratto che ’nteser le parole crude.
Here, the woolly cheeks grew still,of the ferryman of the livid swamp,who had wheels of flame around his eyes.But those souls, who were weary and naked,changed color and gnashed their teeth,as soon as they heard the cruel words.
Bestemmiavano Dio e lor parenti, l’umana spezie e ’l loco e ’l tempo e ’l seme di lor semenza e di lor nascimenti. Poi si ritrasser tutte quante insieme, forte piangendo, a la riva malvagia ch’attende ciascun uom che Dio non teme.
They blasphemed God and their parents, the human race, the place, the time, and the seed of their conception and of their birth. Then they all drew together, weeping loudly, toward the wretched shore that awaits every man who does not fear God.
Caron dimonio, con occhi di bragia loro accennando, tutte le raccoglie; batte col remo qualunque s’adagia. Come d’autunno si levan le foglie l’una appresso de l’altra, fin che ’l ramo vede a la terra tutte le sue spoglie,
The demon Caron, with eyes like coals, beckoned them, gathering them all; he strikes with his oar anyone who delays. Just as in autumn the leaves detach themselves, one after another, until the branch sees all its spoils fallen to the ground,
similemente il mal seme d’Adamo gittansi di quel lito ad una ad una, per cenni come augel per suo richiamo. Così sen vanno su per l’onda bruna, e avanti che sien di là discese, anche di qua nuova schiera s’auna.
Similarly, the wicked seed of Adamcast themselves from that shore one by one,at signals, as a bird is called by its mate.So they move on, up the dark wave,and before they have disembarked on the other side,a new throng gathers on this one.
«Figliuol mio», disse ’l maestro cortese, «quelli che muoion ne l’ira di Dio tutti convegnon qui d’ogne paese; e pronti sono a trapassar lo rio, ché la divina giustizia li sprona, sì che la tema si volve in disio.
"My son," the kind master said, "those who die in God's wrath gather here from every land. They are eager to cross the river, for divine justice spurs them on, so that their fear turns into desire."
Quinci non passa mai anima buona; e però, se Caron di te si lagna, ben puoi sapere omai che ’l suo dir suona». Finito questo, la buia campagna tremò sì forte, che de lo spavento la mente di sudore ancor mi bagna.
No soul that is good ever passes here; therefore, if Charon complains of you, you can well know now what his words mean." When he had finished, the dark land trembled so violently that the memory of it still bathes my mind in sweat.
La terra lagrimosa diede vento, che balenò una luce vermiglia la qual mi vinse ciascun sentimento; e caddi come l’uom cui sonno piglia.
The tear-soaked earth unleashed a windthat flashed a crimson light,which overcame every sense of mine,and I fell as a man overcome by sleep.

A heavy thunderclap broke the deep sleep in my head, so that I started up as if awakened by force.

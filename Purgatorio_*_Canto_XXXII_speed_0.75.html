Dante is staring at Beatrice when he is told to look away. The procession moves to a tall, leafless tree, where the griffin ties the chariot to its trunk. The tree immediately bursts into leaf and flower. As a hymn is sung, Dante falls asleep. He is awakened by Matelda and sees that the procession has gone, leaving Beatrice to guard the chariot under the tree. Beatrice instructs Dante to watch the chariot closely. An eagle descends, striking the tree and the chariot. Next, a fox appears in the chariot but is driven off by Beatrice. The eagle returns, leaving the chariot covered in its feathers. The earth opens and a dragon emerges, thrusting its tail through the chariot's floor and pulling a part of it away. The chariot transforms, sprouting seven monstrous heads. A harlot sits atop it, accompanied by a giant. When the harlot looks at Dante, the giant becomes jealous, beats her, unties the monster, and drags them both into the forest.

The glory of the One who moves all things penetrates the universe and shines in one part more and in another less. In the heaven that receives the most light, I have been, and have seen things that whoever descends from there has neither the knowledge nor the power to speak of. For as our intellect, drawing near its desire, plunges so deep that memory cannot follow, even so, from that point on, my memory fails. Nonetheless, what I was able to treasure of the holy kingdom, I will make my poem.Therefore, let the reader who hears it follow me; and if the grace of God gives him a taste, he will see the blessedness.As the sun, which appears to us here, enters the horizon, so that the image, which it makes in the sky, changes light and shadow, and because of the impact, I was prepared to enter the sun, with such a mixture of new and old, that to this day, my eyes have not seen anything like it.It is believed that the beginning of all things took place by the act of God. The heaven moved by Him, turns with His light; only those who believe have faith. And as the heaven turns, so also, does the light, and it is a marvel to see. And once it had turned in the circular motion, I saw the motion that seemed to be the sun.No mortal would be able to know the state of mind that I was in, as I beheld it. For the things that are above us are beyond our knowledge. I entered into the highest heaven.

Paradiso * Canto IX

After the soul of Charles Martel departs, another luminous spirit approaches Dante. With Beatrice’s silent approval, Dante requests that the soul identify itself. The spirit reveals she is Cunizza da Romano and describes her birthplace in the March of Treviso. She then utters a series of prophecies regarding political violence and corruption in Padua, Treviso, and Feltre. After speaking, she rejoins the other revolving souls. A third spirit, shining intensely, then becomes visible. Dante addresses it, and the soul identifies himself as Folco of Marseilles. He speaks of his past life governed by passionate love. Folco then points to another soul, identifying her as Rahab of Jericho, explaining her presence in this heaven. He concludes his discourse by denouncing the avarice of the church, specifically the Pope and cardinals, and prophesying that the Vatican will soon be freed from their corruption.

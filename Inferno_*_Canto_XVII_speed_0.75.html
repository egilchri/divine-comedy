Inferno * Canto XVII

At the edge of the seventh circle, Virgil summons a beast named Geryon. The creature arrives, presenting the face of a just man, the body of a serpent covered in intricate patterns, two hairy paws, and a scorpion’s stinger at the end of its tail. While Virgil arranges their passage, he sends Dante to observe a group of souls sitting nearby on the burning sand. These sinners are weeping, and each has a pouch with a distinct family crest hanging from their neck. One soul addresses Dante before the poet returns to his guide. Dante finds Virgil already seated on Geryon’s back. He instructs a terrified Dante to mount in front of him for protection. Virgil then commands Geryon, who begins a slow, spiraling descent into the abyss of the eighth circle. During the flight, Dante becomes aware of the fires and laments below. After landing the two poets at the base of the great cliff, Geryon vanishes.
«Ecco la fiera con la coda aguzza, che passa i monti e rompe i muri e l’armi! Ecco colei che tutto ’l mondo appuzza!».
"Behold the beast with the sharpened tail,who crosses mountains and shatters walls and weapons! Behold the one that makes the whole world stink!"
Sì cominciò lo mio duca a parlarmi; e accennolle che venisse a proda, vicino al fin d’i passeggiati marmi. E quella sozza imagine di froda sen venne, e arrivò la testa e ’l busto, ma ’n su la riva non trasse la coda.
Then my guide began to speak to me, and gestured for it to come to the shore, near the end of the traversed marble. And that foul image of fraud approached, and brought its head and chest, but did not drag its tail onto the bank.
La faccia sua era faccia d’uom giusto, tanto benigna avea di fuor la pelle, e d’un serpente tutto l’altro fusto; due branche avea pilose insin l’ascelle; lo dosso e ’l petto e ambedue le coste dipinti avea di nodi e di rotelle.
Its face was the face of a just man, so benign did its skin appear from the outside, while the rest of its form was that of a serpent. It had two hairy paws reaching up to its armpits; its back, chest, and both sides were painted with knots and small circles.
Con più color, sommesse e sovraposte non fer mai drappi Tartari né Turchi, né fuor tai tele per Aragne imposte. Come talvolta stanno a riva i burchi, che parte sono in acqua e parte in terra, e come là tra li Tedeschi lurchi
With colors more numerous, subdued, and layered, never did Tartar or Turk weave fabrics like these, nor were such webs spun by Arachne. Like barges sometimes rest on the shore, with part in the water and part on land, and like amongst those boorish Germans...
lo bivero s’assetta a far sua guerra, così la fiera pessima si stava su l’orlo ch’è di pietra e ’l sabbion serra. Nel vano tutta sua coda guizzava, torcendo in sù la venenosa forca ch’a guisa di scorpion la punta armava.
The evil beast settled in to wage its war, just as the vile creature perched upon the edge made of stone, which held the sand. In the empty space, its entire tail writhed, twisting upwards the poisonous fork which, like a scorpion, armed its point.
Lo duca disse: «Or convien che si torca la nostra via un poco insino a quella bestia malvagia che colà si corca». Però scendemmo a la destra mammella, e diece passi femmo in su lo stremo, per ben cessar la rena e la fiammella.
The guide said, "Now it is necessary that our path swerve a bit towards that vile beast that lies there." Therefore, we descended to the right slope, and took ten steps along the edge, in order to avoid the sand and the flames.
E quando noi a lei venuti semo, poco più oltre veggio in su la rena gente seder propinqua al loco scemo. Quivi ’l maestro «Acciò che tutta piena esperïenza d’esto giron porti», mi disse, «va, e vedi la lor mena.
And when we came to her, I saw, a little farther on, upon the sand, a crowd of people seated close to the place of torment. There the master said, "So that you may carry away a complete experience of this circle," he said to me, "go and observe their ways."
Li tuoi ragionamenti sian là corti; mentre che torni, parlerò con questa, che ne conceda i suoi omeri forti». Così ancor su per la strema testa di quel settimo cerchio tutto solo andai, dove sedea la gente mesta.
Let your words be brief there. While you return, I will speak with her, that she may grant us her strong shoulders.” And so, alone, I went still farther along the edge of the seventh circle, where the mournful people sat.
Per li occhi fora scoppiava lor duolo; di qua, di là soccorrien con le mani quando a’ vapori, e quando al caldo suolo: non altrimenti fan di state i cani or col ceffo or col piè, quando son morsi o da pulci o da mosche o da tafani.
From their eyes, their pain was bursting forth; here and there, they sought relief with their hands, now from the vapors, now from the burning ground. In the same way, dogs do in summer, now with their muzzles, now with their paws, when bitten by fleas or flies or horseflies.
Poi che nel viso a certi li occhi porsi, ne’ quali ’l doloroso foco casca, non ne conobbi alcun; ma io m’accorsi che dal collo a ciascun pendea una tasca ch’avea certo colore e certo segno, e quindi par che ’l loro occhio si pasca.
Then, focusing my gaze on the faces of some, into whose eyes the painful fire fell, I recognized no one. But I noticed that from each neck hung a pouch, of a specific color and with a certain emblem, and from that, it seemed, their eyes were feeding.
E com’ io riguardando tra lor vegno, in una borsa gialla vidi azzurro che d’un leone avea faccia e contegno. Poi, procedendo di mio sguardo il curro, vidine un’altra come sangue rossa, mostrando un’oca bianca più che burro.
And as I looked among them, I saw blue on a yellow pouch, which had the face and form of a lion. Then, letting my gaze proceed, I saw another, red as blood, showing a goose whiter than butter.
E un che d’una scrofa azzurra e grossa segnato avea lo suo sacchetto bianco, mi disse: «Che fai tu in questa fossa? Or te ne va; e perché se’ vivo anco, sappi che ’l mio vicin Vitalïano sederà qui dal mio sinistro fianco.
And one whose white pouch was marked with a large, blue sow said to me: "What do you do in this pit? Go now; and because you are still alive, know that my neighbor Vitaliano will sit here on my left."
Con questi Fiorentin son padoano: spesse fïate mi ’ntronan li orecchi gridando: “Vegna ’l cavalier sovrano, che recherà la tasca con tre becchi!”».
With these Florentines I am a Paduan. Often they deafen my ears, crying: "Let the sovereign knight come, who will bring the purse with three beaks!"
Qui distorse la bocca e di fuor trasse la lingua, come bue che ’l naso lecchi. E io, temendo no ’l più star crucciasse lui che di poco star m’avea ’mmonito, torna’mi in dietro da l’anime lasse.
He twisted his mouth and stuck out his tongue, like an ox licking its nose. And I, fearing that he who had warned me against lingering would grow more angry, turned back from the weary souls.
Trova’ il duca mio ch’era salito già su la groppa del fiero animale, e disse a me: «Or sie forte e ardito. Omai si scende per sì fatte scale; monta dinanzi, ch’i’ voglio esser mezzo, sì che la coda non possa far male».
I found my guide, who had already mounted onto the back of the fierce beast. He said to me, "Now be strong and brave. We descend by such stairs as these; you go in front, for I wish to be in the middle, so that the tail cannot harm you."
Qual è colui che sì presso ha ’l riprezzo de la quartana, c’ha già l’unghie smorte, e triema tutto pur guardando ’l rezzo, tal divenn’ io a le parole porte; ma vergogna mi fé le sue minacce, che innanzi a buon segnor fa servo forte.
I became like a man in the grip of a fever, whose nails are already pale, and who shivers at the very thought of a shadow. His threats made me ashamed, for a servant is emboldened in the presence of a good master.
I’ m’assettai in su quelle spallacce; sì volli dir, ma la voce non venne com’ io credetti: ‘Fa che tu m’abbracce’. Ma esso, ch’altra volta mi sovvenne ad altro forse, tosto ch’i’ montai con le braccia m’avvinse e mi sostenne;
I settled myself on those broad shoulders. I wanted to speak, but the voice did not come as I had intended: "Make sure you embrace me." But he, who had aided me another time, perhaps for something else, as soon as I mounted him, he clasped me with his arms and supported me.
e disse: «Gerïon, moviti omai: le rote larghe, e lo scender sia poco; pensa la nova soma che tu hai». Come la navicella esce di loco in dietro in dietro, sì quindi si tolse; e poi ch’al tutto si sentì a gioco,
And he said, "Gerion, get moving now: make the circles wide, and the descent be gradual; consider the new weight that you bear." As a small boat moves away from shore, backing away, so did he then depart; and once he felt he was completely in control,
là ’v’ era ’l petto, la coda rivolse, e quella tesa, come anguilla, mosse, e con le branche l’aere a sé raccolse. Maggior paura non credo che fosse quando Fetonte abbandonò li freni, per che ’l ciel, come pare ancor, si cosse;
There, where the chest had been, the tail turned, and, taut, like an eel, it moved, and with its claws gathered the air toward itself. Greater fear, I believe, was never felt than when Phaeton abandoned the reins, causing the sky, as still seems apparent, to scorch.
né quando Icaro misero le reni sentì spennar per la scaldata cera, gridando il padre a lui «Mala via tieni!», che fu la mia, quando vidi ch’i’ era ne l’aere d’ogne parte, e vidi spenta ogne veduta fuor che de la fera.
Nor when wretched Icarus felt his flanks losing feathers to the melted wax, as his father cried to him, "You're on the wrong path!", did a greater fear consume me than when I saw I was in air on all sides, and all vision was extinguished save that of the beast.
Ella sen va notando lenta lenta; rota e discende, ma non me n’accorgo se non che al viso e di sotto mi venta. Io sentia già da la man destra il gorgo far sotto noi un orribile scroscio, per che con li occhi ’n giù la testa sporgo.
She drifted away slowly, slowly; broken and descending, but I didn't notice, except that it blew on my face and underneath me. I could already hear the whirlpool on the right making a horrible crash beneath us, so I lowered my head and looked down.
Allor fu’ io più timido a lo stoscio, però ch’i’ vidi fuochi e senti’ pianti; ond’ io tremando tutto mi raccoscio. E vidi poi, ché nol vedea davanti, lo scendere e ’l girar per li gran mali che s’appressavan da diversi canti.
Then I became more fearful at the descent, because I saw fires and heard wails; so, trembling all over, I huddled close. And afterwards I saw, though I hadn't seen it before, the descent and the circling through the great evils that approached from different directions.
Come ’l falcon ch’è stato assai su l’ali, che sanza veder logoro o uccello fa dire al falconiere «Omè, tu cali!», discende lasso onde si move isnello, per cento rote, e da lunge si pone dal suo maestro, disdegnoso e fello;
Like a falcon that has soared long on its wings, which without seeing lure or bird makes the falconer say, "Alas, you descend!", it descends wearily from where it was nimble, through a hundred circles, and settles far from its master, disdainful and fierce.
così ne puose al fondo Gerïone al piè al piè de la stagliata rocca, e, discarcate le nostre persone, si dileguò come da corda cocca.
Thus he placed us at the bottom, Gerion, at the foot, foot of the jagged rock; and, having unloaded our persons, he vanished as a bolt from a string.

A place in Hell is called Malebolge, all made of iron-colored stone, like the circle that surrounds it.

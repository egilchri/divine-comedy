As Dante and Virgil cross the bank between the eighth and ninth circles, a loud horn blast sounds. In the dim light, Dante mistakes a series of tall shapes for towers, but Virgil corrects him, explaining they are Giants, standing in a central pit from the navel down. They first approach Nimrod, who yells incomprehensible phrases. Virgil identifies him and they proceed. They then see Ephialtes, bound by a heavy chain, who shakes violently when mentioned. Virgil informs Dante that Briareus is also nearby and similarly bound. They reach the unchained giant Antaeus. Virgil persuades him to lower them to the final circle by promising that Dante can spread his fame on Earth. Antaeus cups the two poets in his giant hand and deposits them onto the frozen surface of Cocytus, the ninth and final circle of Hell.

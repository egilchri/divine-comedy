Paradiso * Canto XIV

Following the silence of Thomas Aquinas in the Sphere of the Sun, Beatrice voices two questions on Dante's behalf to the surrounding souls. She asks if their enveloping light will remain with them eternally and, if so, how their vision will endure it after they are reunited with their physical bodies. The two circles of spirits dance and sing joyfully. A voice, identified as King Solomon, responds from the inner circle. He affirms that their light will last forever and will increase after the resurrection, when their glorified bodies will be strong enough to perceive the heightened brilliance. The souls express their eager assent. Suddenly, a third, brighter circle of spirits materializes around the other two, overwhelming Dante’s sight. Beatrice’s smile restores him, and he finds they have ascended to the Fifth Heaven, the Sphere of Mars, which glows with a deep red color. Here, countless shining souls are arranged in the form of a vast, brilliant cross. These spirits move along its form, and a melody emanates from them which Dante hears as a hymn of praise.
Dal centro al cerchio, e sì dal cerchio al centro movesi l’acqua in un ritondo vaso, secondo ch’è percosso fuori o dentro: ne la mia mente fé sùbito caso questo ch’io dico, sì come si tacque la glorïosa vita di Tommaso,
From center to circle, and then from circle to center, the water moves in a round vessel, depending on whether it’s struck from the outside or inside: in my mind, this that I say immediately made sense, as soon as the glorious life of Thomas fell silent.

Because of the similarity that arosebetween his words and those of Beatrice,who, after him, it pleased to begin: "This one needs, and he does not tell youeither with his voice or even by thinking,to go to the root of another truth."

Tell them if the light that makes your essence bloomwill remain with you eternally, just as it is now;and if it remains, say how, then,when you are made visible again,it will be so that it does not wound our sight."
Come, da più letizia pinti e tratti, a la fïata quei che vanno a rota levan la voce e rallegrano li atti, così, a l’orazion pronta e divota, li santi cerchi mostrar nova gioia nel torneare e ne la mira nota.
Come, pushed and drawn by greater joy, those who circle in a ring raise their voices and rejoice in their movements; so, at the ready and devout prayer, the holy circles showed new joy in their turning and in the marvelous sight.

So swift and eager did each choir appear, both of them crying out "Amen!" that they clearly showed desire for their resurrected bodies; perhaps not only for themselves, but for their mothers, fathers, and others who were dear to them before they became eternal flames.

Ben m’accors’ io ch’elli era d’alte lode, però ch’a me venìa «Resurgi» e «Vinci» come a colui che non intende e ode. Ïo m’innamorava tanto quinci, che ’nfino a lì non fu alcuna cosa che mi legasse con sì dolci vinci.
Then I understood that it was of high praise, because "Rise again" and "Conquer" came to me, as to one who does not understand and yet hears. I was so enamored here, that until then there was nothing that bound me with such sweet chains.

He began: "There is nothing here that the mountain's faith does not feel, nor anything that lies outside its custom. Here is freedom from all alteration: We are the cause of what the heavens receive from themselves, and nothing more."

And the wise leader: "Now I see the net that ensnares you here and how it breaks apart, because it trembles for us, and what you celebrate. Now, who were you, please let me know, and why, having lain here for so many centuries, are you, let me understand through your words."

To my ardor were the sparks, which warmed me, of the divine flame, whence more than a thousand are illuminated. I speak of the Aeneid, which was my mother, and nourished me, in poetry: without it, I did not fix the weight of a dram.

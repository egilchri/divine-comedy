Purgatorio * Canto XVIII

Virgil concludes his lengthy explanation of love and free will to Dante. As night deepens, Dante grows drowsy but is abruptly roused by the sound of a large group of spirits running furiously along the terrace. These are the souls purging the sin of sloth. Two spirits at the head of the crowd shout examples of zeal, citing the Virgin Mary and Julius Caesar. Virgil calls out to them, asking for the location of the stairway to the next level. One of the runners, identifying himself as a former abbot of San Zeno in Verona, tells them to follow the crowd to find the opening, then quickly rushes on. As the main group passes, Virgil directs Dante’s attention to two spirits at the rear who are shouting examples of sloth’s negative consequences. After the crowd disappears from sight, Dante’s mind drifts through various thoughts until he is overcome by sleep and begins to dream.

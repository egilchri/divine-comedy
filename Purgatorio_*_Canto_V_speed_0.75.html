As Dante follows Virgil up the mountain, souls behind them notice that Dante casts a shadow, indicating he is alive. Virgil urges him not to be distracted. They soon encounter another group singing the "Miserere," who also see the shadow and send two messengers to inquire. Virgil confirms Dante’s living state. This crowd of spirits, all of whom died violently but repented at the last moment, surrounds the poets, asking Dante to carry news of them to the world. Three speak individually. Jacopo del Cassero recounts his assassination and asks for prayers. Bonconte da Montefeltro describes dying at the Battle of Campaldino; an angel saved his soul from a demon, which then unleashed a storm that washed his body into the Arno river. Finally, a woman named Pia asks Dante to remember her when he returns to earth and has rested from his journey.

I had already departed from that shade and was following the tracks of my guide when, from behind me, someone shouted, pointing a finger: "Look, the light does not seem to shine down there from the left, and he seems to move like a living being!"

Vapori accesi non vid’ io sì tosto di prima notte mai fender sereno, né, sol calando, nuvole d’agosto, che color non tornasser suso in meno; e, giunti là, con li altri a noi dier volta, come schiera che scorre sanza freno.
I never saw vapors, ignited so swiftly, cleave the sky on the first night, nor, as the sun descended, clouds of August, that did not return upward in less time; and, having arrived there, they turned toward us, with the others, like a host that runs without restraint.

"This crowd that presses us is great, and comes to beg of you," the poet said, "but still, go on, and as you walk, listen." "O soul who goes to be made joyous with those limbs with which you were born," they cried, "slow your step a bit."
Guarda s’alcun di noi unqua vedesti, sì che di lui di là novella porti: deh, perché vai? deh, perché non t’arresti?

Noi fummo tutti già per forza morti, e peccatori infino a l’ultima ora; quivi lume del ciel ne fece accorti, sì che, pentendo e perdonando, fora di vita uscimmo a Dio pacificati, che del disio di sé veder n’accora».
We were all dead by violence, and sinners up until our final hour; heaven's light made us aware there, so that, repenting and pardoning, we departed from life, at peace with God, who stirs our longing to see Him.”
E io: «Perché ne’ vostri visi guati, non riconosco alcun; ma s’a voi piace cosa ch’io possa, spiriti ben nati, voi dite, e io farò per quella pace che, dietro a’ piedi di sì fatta guida, di mondo in mondo cercar mi si face».
And I said: "Because I scrutinize your faces, I recognize none of you; but if it pleases you, well-born spirits, to say something I can do, tell me, and I will do it, for the sake of the peace that, following the footsteps of such a guide, compels me to search from world to world."

Io fui di Montefeltro, io son Bonconte; Giovanna o altri non ha di me cura; per ch’io vo tra costor con bassa fronte». E io a lui: «Qual forza o qual ventura ti travïò sì fuor di Campaldino, che non si seppe mai tua sepultura?».

Inferno * Canto XXIV

Dante and Virgil begin by climbing the steep ruins of a collapsed bridge leading to the seventh pouch of the eighth circle. The ascent is physically taxing, and Dante is exhausted upon reaching the top. Virgil admonishes him to continue the difficult journey without rest. From the next ridge, they look down into a dark ditch filled with a horrifying mass of serpents. Naked sinners run terrified among them. Their hands are bound behind their backs by snakes that bite them. Dante witnesses one sinner, Vanni Fucci, being bitten in the neck. The man instantly incinerates into ash, which then gathers and reforms back into his human shape. When questioned by Virgil, Fucci confesses he is a thief, punished for stealing holy furnishings from a sacristy in Pistoia. Shamed at being recognized by Dante, Fucci delivers a spiteful prophecy predicting the future defeat of Dante’s political party.

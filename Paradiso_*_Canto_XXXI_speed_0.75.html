Paradiso * Canto XXXI

In the Empyrean, Dante beholds the souls of the saved arranged in the form of a vast, white rose. Angels with flame-like faces and golden wings fly between the tiers of the rose and the light of God above. When Dante turns to speak with Beatrice, he finds she is gone and an elderly man now stands beside him. The man explains that Beatrice has returned to her throne and sent him to guide Dante on the final part of his journey. Following the man’s direction, Dante looks up and sees Beatrice seated in the third highest tier of the rose. He offers her a prayer of thanks; she smiles at him and then turns back toward the divine light. The old man reveals he is Saint Bernard and instructs Dante to look at the garden of souls, focusing on its highest point. There, Dante sees the Virgin Mary, surrounded by more than a thousand celebrating angels.

Inferno * Canto VIII

As Dante and Virgil approach a tall tower, they see signal fires answered from a distance. A skiff, piloted by the boatman Phlegyas, rapidly crosses the marsh of Styx to meet them. Phlegyas challenges their presence, but Virgil rebukes him, and they board the vessel. During the crossing, a soul covered in mud, Filippo Argenti, rises from the water and confronts Dante. Following a hostile exchange, Virgil shoves Argenti away. At Dante's wish, other wrathful souls in the swamp then converge on Argenti and tear him apart. They arrive at the walls of the city of Dis. A host of fallen angels guards the gate and refuses them passage. Virgil attempts to negotiate, but the demons slam the gate shut in his face. He returns to the waiting Dante, frustrated but assuring him that a powerful entity is on its way to force their entry.

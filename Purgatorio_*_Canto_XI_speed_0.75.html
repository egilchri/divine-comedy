Purgatorio * Canto XI

On the first terrace of Purgatory, Dante and Virgil encounter the souls of the proud, who are forced to crawl while carrying immense stones on their backs. The canto begins with these souls reciting a paraphrase of the Lord's Prayer. Virgil asks them for the easiest path to ascend, and a voice directs them to the right. The speaker, unable to lift his head, introduces himself as Omberto Aldobrandesco, who is atoning for his arrogance over his noble birth. As Dante stoops to walk alongside the penitents, he is recognized by another soul, the famous manuscript illuminator Oderisi of Gubbio. Oderisi laments the fleeting nature of earthly fame, citing his own art being surpassed by another’s. He then points to a third figure, Provenzan Salvani, a Sienese leader. When Dante asks why Salvani is not in Ante-Purgatory despite his late repentance, Oderisi explains it is because he humbled himself by publicly begging to ransom a friend.

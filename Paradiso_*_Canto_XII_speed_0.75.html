Paradiso * Canto XII

As the first ring of spirits, led by Thomas Aquinas, ceases its song and rotation, a second, concentric ring of twelve spirits appears. This new circle mirrors the first in motion and song. From this second group, a voice emerges. The speaker, a Franciscan, begins by delivering a eulogy for Saint Dominic, detailing his life, his mission against heresy, and his devotion. Following the praise for Dominic, the speaker laments the state of his own Franciscan order, stating that its members have deviated from their founder's path. He then reveals his identity as Bonaventure of Bagnoregio and proceeds to name the other eleven spirits in his circle. These include figures such as Illuminato, Augustine, Hugh of Saint Victor, Nathan the prophet, Anselm, and Joachim of Fiore. Bonaventure concludes by stating that he was moved to speak by the courtesy of Thomas Aquinas in the previous canto.
